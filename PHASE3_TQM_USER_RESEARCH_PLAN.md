1. **Quality Audits Module** (Priority 1 - Q2 2026)

- **Methods:** Semi-structured interviews (60-90 min), workflow shadowing (2-4 hrs), co-design workshops (3 hrs)
- **Timeline:** 4 weeks (January 13 - February 7, 2026)

## SAtSD Principles Applied to User Research

### **Principle 1: Focus on people and their experiences**
**Application:**
- Quality managers are primary users of TQM modules (not just system administrators)
- Research explores their day-to-day workflows, frustrations, and workarounds
- Understanding emotional journey: stress during inspections, satisfaction from closed-loop QI

**Research Questions:**
- What does a typical day look like for a quality manager in a care home?
- What tasks are most time-consuming? Most stressful? Most rewarding?
- How do you currently prepare for Care Inspectorate inspections?

---

### **Principle 2: Be inclusive throughout**
**Application:**
- Recruit diverse participants: new quality managers (<1 year) + experienced (3+ years)
- Include managers from different home sizes (small 20-bed vs. large 60-bed)
- Consider managers with varying IT literacy (digital natives vs. paper-first)

**Recruitment Strategy:**
- Target 3-5 participants across current 5 care homes
- Ensure representation: 1 new manager, 2 mid-experience, 1 senior
- Diversity in inspection history: recent inspections (last 6 months) + upcoming inspections

---

### **Principle 3: Collaborate and co-design**
**Application:**
- Participatory design workshops where managers sketch workflows and interfaces
- Iterative prototyping: show mockups â†’ gather feedback â†’ refine â†’ repeat
- Managers become co-creators, not just interviewees

**Co-Design Activities:**
- Workflow mapping sessions (current state vs. ideal future state)
- Paper prototyping of TQM module screens
- Feature prioritization exercises (MoSCoW method: Must/Should/Could/Won't)

---

### **Principle 4: Build on what works, and challenge what doesn't**
**Application:**
- Identify strengths in current quality systems (manual processes that work well)
- Challenge inefficiencies (duplicate data entry, lost documentation, reactive incident response)
- Leverage existing staff rota system foundations (audit trail, role-based access)

**Research Questions:**
- What aspects of your current quality management system work well?
- What frustrates you most about current processes?
- If you could wave a magic wand, what would you change?

---

### **Principle 5: Start with the whole picture, then focus on specifics**
**Application:**
- Begin research understanding overall quality management ecosystem (not just one module)
- Map interdependencies: How do audits inform training needs? How do incidents trigger policy updates?
- Then drill down into specific TQM module workflows

**Research Sequence:**
1. **Session 1:** Big picture quality management (60 min)
2. **Session 2:** Deep dive on top 2 priority modules (90 min)
3. **Session 3:** Co-design workshop on specific features (3 hrs)

---

### **Principle 6: Work iteratively, with feedback loops**
**Application:**
- User research â†’ Design â†’ Prototype â†’ Test â†’ Refine (repeat)
- Show early wireframes to participants for feedback before coding
- Beta testing with same quality managers post-development

**Feedback Loops:**
- Week 1: Interviews + initial findings synthesis
- Week 2: Wireframe review sessions (30 min each participant)
- Week 3: Revised prototypes + feature prioritization
- Week 4: Final specifications review + user acceptance criteria

---

### **Principle 7: Be evidence-led and data-informed**
**Application:**
- Quantify current pain points (hours spent on quality tasks, inspection prep time)
- Use system usage data to inform priorities (which reports are most accessed?)
- Evidence-based decision making on feature scope (ROI analysis)

**Data Collection:**
- Time-motion study: How long do current quality tasks take?
- Error rate analysis: How often are mistakes made in manual quality processes?
- Compliance metrics: What % of quality indicators currently have evidence?

## Double Diamond Framework for TQM Co-Design

### **DISCOVER Phase (Week 1: January 13-17)**

**Objective:** Understand the problem space and user context

**Activities:**

**1. Participant Recruitment (Day 1: Monday January 13)**
- Send recruitment emails to 5 care home quality managers
- Screening criteria:
  - Responsible for Care Inspectorate compliance at their home
  - Minimum 6 months in quality management role
  - Experience with manual quality systems (understand pain points)
  - Available for 60-90 min interview + 3-hour workshop
- Target: Confirm 3-5 participants by end of day

**2. Semi-Structured Interviews (Days 2-3: Tuesday-Wednesday January 14-15)**
- 60-90 minute interviews with each participant
- Location: On-site at their care home (observe natural environment)
- Interview guide topics:
  - Current quality management workflows
  - Pain points and frustrations
  - Regulatory compliance challenges (CI, HIS, NES)
  - Inspection preparation processes
  - Incident management workflows
  - Audit scheduling and documentation
  - Feedback/complaint handling
  - Training compliance tracking
  - Document version control
- Recording: Audio + handwritten notes (consent obtained)

**3. Workflow Shadowing (Days 4-5: Thursday-Friday January 16-17)**
- 2-4 hours observing quality managers in their daily work
- Observe tasks:
  - Conducting quality audits (if scheduled)
  - Incident investigation and documentation
  - Preparing for upcoming inspection
  - Reviewing compliance reports
  - Responding to staff questions about quality procedures
- Capture screenshots of current systems/tools used
- Document workarounds and manual processes

**Deliverable:** Discovery findings report (5-10 pages) synthesizing themes, pain points, user needs

---

### **DEFINE Phase (Week 2: January 20-24)**

**Objective:** Define the problem statement and prioritize opportunities

**Activities:**

**1. Findings Synthesis (Days 1-2: Monday-Tuesday January 20-21)**
- Analyze interview transcripts and observation notes
- Affinity mapping: Cluster insights into themes
- Identify patterns across participants
- Quantify pain points (time wasted, error rates, stress levels)

**Synthesis Framework:**
- **User Personas:** Create 2-3 quality manager personas (e.g., "Experienced Emma," "New Nathan," "Overwhelmed Olivia")
- **User Journey Maps:** Current state journey for key tasks (conducting audit, investigating incident, preparing for inspection)
- **Pain Point Ranking:** Sort by frequency Ã— severity
- **Opportunity Areas:** Where can TQM modules add most value?

**2. Wireframe Development (Days 3-4: Wednesday-Thursday January 22-23)**
- Create low-fidelity wireframes for top 2 priority TQM modules
- Based on user needs identified in discovery
- Focus on core workflows (not every feature)
- Tools: Balsamiq, Figma, or hand-drawn sketches

**Wireframe Screens (Quality Audits Module example):**
- Audit schedule calendar view
- Create new audit workflow (template selection, scheduling, assignment)
- Audit checklist interface (mobile-optimized for on-the-go audits)
- Findings documentation (non-compliances, observations, photos)
- Action tracking (assign corrective actions, deadlines, follow-up)
- Audit report generation

**3. Wireframe Review Sessions (Day 5: Friday January 24)**
- 30-minute sessions with each participant
- Show wireframes, gather feedback
- Questions:
  - Does this workflow make sense?
  - What's missing?
  - What would you change?
  - Would you use this feature? Why/why not?
- Iterate wireframes based on feedback

**Deliverable:** Refined wireframes + problem statement document ("Quality managers need a way to...")

---

### **DEVELOP Phase (Week 3: January 27-31)**

**Objective:** Ideate solutions and develop detailed specifications

**Activities:**

**1. Co-Design Workshop (Day 1: Tuesday January 28, 3 hours)**
- Bring all 3-5 participants together
- Location: Central office or largest care home (meeting room)
- Agenda:
  - **Hour 1: Feature Prioritization Exercise**
    - Present all potential TQM features identified
    - MoSCoW voting (Must have, Should have, Could have, Won't have)
    - Dot voting on top priorities
  - **Hour 2: Workflow Design Session**
    - Small groups (2-3 people) sketch ideal workflows
    - Paper prototyping: Draw screens on sticky notes
    - Walk through scenario: "You've just had a resident fall incident, walk me through how you'd document it in the new system"
  - **Hour 3: Interface Refinement**
    - Group reviews each team's workflows
    - Identify common patterns vs. divergent preferences
    - Converge on preferred approach
- Outputs: Annotated sketches, prioritized feature list, workflow diagrams

**2. Technical Feasibility Assessment (Days 2-3: Wednesday-Thursday January 29-30)**
- Review co-designed features with development team
- Assess complexity (effort estimates in story points)
- Identify technical dependencies (e.g., mobile app required for on-the-go audits)
- Flag potential challenges (e.g., offline mode for audits in areas with poor Wi-Fi)

**3. Specification Drafting (Day 4: Friday January 31)**
- Write detailed user stories for top 10 priority features
- Format: "As a [quality manager], I want to [action], so that [benefit]"
- Include acceptance criteria, wireframes, workflow diagrams
- Estimate effort and assign to sprint backlogs

**Deliverable:** TQM module specifications (20-30 pages per module) with prioritized feature backlog

## Interview Guide - Semi-Structured Interviews

### **Introduction (5 minutes)**

**Welcome & Consent:**
"Thank you for participating in this research to co-design the Total Quality Management modules for our staff rota system. Your insights as a quality manager are invaluable to ensuring we build features that truly meet your needs and make your work easier.

This interview will take 60-90 minutes. I'll ask questions about your current quality management processes, pain points, and ideal solutions. There are no right or wrong answers - I want to understand your real-world experiences.

With your permission, I'd like to audio record this session to ensure I capture everything accurately. The recording will only be used for this research and will be kept confidential. Are you comfortable with that?"

**[Obtain consent, start recording]**

**Background Questions:**
- How long have you been a quality manager at this care home?
- What attracted you to quality management in care homes?
- What relevant training/qualifications do you have?

---

### **Section 1: Current Quality Management Landscape (15 minutes)**

**Big Picture:**
1. "Walk me through a typical week as a quality manager. What are your main responsibilities?"
2. "What quality management systems or tools do you currently use?" (Prompts: Paper-based? Excel? Third-party software?)
3. "How would you describe the biggest challenge in managing quality in a care home?"

**Regulatory Context:**
4. "How often do you interact with Care Inspectorate requirements?" (Daily? Weekly? Only during inspections?)
5. "What HIS or NES frameworks do you actively use in your quality improvement work?" (QMS, PDSA, Model for Improvement?)
6. "When was your last Care Inspectorate inspection? How did that go?"

**Pain Points:**
7. "If I followed you around for a day, what tasks would I see you doing that feel like a waste of time or unnecessarily difficult?"
8. "What keeps you up at night as a quality manager?" (Prompts: Upcoming inspection? Missing evidence? Staff compliance?)

---

### **Section 2: Quality Audits (15 minutes)**

**Current Process:**
9. "Tell me about how you currently conduct quality audits. Walk me through the process from start to finish."
   - How do you decide what to audit and when?
   - How do you schedule audits?
   - What tools do you use during audits? (Paper checklists? Tablets? Photos?)
   - How do you document findings?
   - How do you track corrective actions?

**Pain Points:**
10. "What frustrates you most about the current audit process?"
11. "Have you ever lost audit documentation or struggled to find past audit reports?" (How often?)
12. "How long does it typically take to conduct one audit and complete the documentation?" (Average in hours/days)

**Ideal State:**
13. "If you could design the perfect audit management system, what would it include?"
14. "What would make audits easier, faster, or more effective?"
15. "How do you currently use audit findings to drive quality improvement?" (Link to PDSA cycles? Board reporting?)

---

### **Section 3: Incident Management (15 minutes)**

**Current Process:**
16. "Walk me through what happens when a resident incident occurs - say, a fall with injury. What are all the steps you take?"
   - Immediate response
   - Documentation (forms, systems)
   - Investigation (who, when, how)
   - Root cause analysis (do you use 5 Whys? Fishbone diagrams?)
   - Action planning
   - Follow-up and closure
   - Reporting (to CI? SSSC? Families?)

**Pain Points:**
17. "What's the most frustrating part of incident management?"
18. "How often do incident investigations get delayed or incomplete?" (Reasons why?)
19. "Do you feel you learn effectively from incidents, or are they just a documentation burden?" (Prompts: Trend analysis? Pattern recognition?)

**Ideal State:**
20. "What would help you investigate incidents more thoroughly and efficiently?"
21. "How would you like to analyze incident trends?" (Prompts: By type? By shift? By staff? By time of day?)
22. "What SPSP (Scottish Patient Safety Programme) principles would you like to see integrated?" (Duty of Candour tracking? Safety culture?)

---

### **Section 4: Feedback & Complaints Management (10 minutes)**

**Current Process:**
23. "How do you currently collect resident and family feedback?"
   - Surveys? Suggestion boxes? Verbal feedback?
   - Frequency?
   - How is it documented?
24. "Walk me through your complaint handling process."
   - Initial logging
   - Investigation
   - Response to complainant
   - Actions taken
   - Closure and learning

**Pain Points:**
25. "What challenges do you face in systematically gathering resident/family feedback?"
26. "How do you demonstrate to Care Inspectorate that you listen and act on feedback?" (Show closed-loop: "You said, we did")

**Ideal State:**
27. "What would make feedback and complaint management easier and more effective?"
28. "How would you like to involve residents and families in quality improvement decisions?"

---

### **Section 5: Training & Competency Tracking (10 minutes)**

**Current Process:**
29. "You already have training compliance tracking in the staff rota system. What works well? What's missing?"
30. "How do you currently track competency levels (not just course completion, but actual proficiency)?"
   - Pre/post assessments?
   - Practical observations?
   - Skills matrices?

**Pain Points:**
31. "What gaps exist in current training management that affect quality?"
32. "How do you identify training needs based on quality issues?" (E.g., if falls increase, do you trigger falls prevention training?)

**Ideal State:**
33. "What enhancements to training tracking would help quality management?"
34. "How would you like to link training to competency-based role assignments?"

---

### **Section 6: Document Control & Policy Management (10 minutes)**

**Current Process:**
35. "How do you manage policies, procedures, and quality documents?"
   - Version control?
   - Staff access?
   - Review schedules?
   - Approval workflows?

**Pain Points:**
36. "Have you ever had a staff member use an outdated policy?" (How did you find out? Consequences?)
37. "How do you ensure everyone has read new/updated policies?" (Sign-off tracking?)

**Ideal State:**
38. "What would make document control easier?" (Prompts: Automatic alerts for reviews? Read receipts? Change history?)

---

### **Section 7: Inspection Preparation (10 minutes)**

**Current Process:**
39. "You mentioned your last inspection was [date]. How long did it take you to prepare the evidence?"
40. "Walk me through your preparation process." (What evidence did you gather? How long to locate it?)

**Pain Points:**
41. "What was most stressful about preparing for inspection?"
42. "What evidence was hardest to find or demonstrate?"

**Ideal State:**
43. "If the system could automatically prepare inspection evidence packs, what would you want included?"
44. "How confident would you feel presenting evidence generated by the new TQM modules to a Care Inspectorate inspector?"

---

### **Section 8: Prioritization & Co-Design (10 minutes)**

**Feature Ranking:**
45. "Of the 5 TQM modules we've discussed (Quality Audits, Incident Management, Feedback, Training, Document Control), which is your top priority?" (Why?)
46. "If you could only have 3 features in the Quality Audits module, what would they be?"
47. "What's one feature you absolutely must have for the system to be useful to you?"

**Co-Design Willingness:**
48. "Would you be willing to participate in a 3-hour co-design workshop to sketch out workflows and interfaces?" (Schedule availability?)
49. "Would you be interested in beta testing the modules once developed?" (Provide feedback before full rollout?)

---

### **Closing (5 minutes)**

**Summary:**
"Thank you so much for sharing your experiences. Just to summarize what I've heard..." [Recap 3-5 key themes]

**Next Steps:**
"I'll be conducting interviews with 2-4 other quality managers this week, then synthesizing all the findings. Next week, I'll share some initial wireframe designs with you for feedback (30-minute session). Does [proposed date/time] work for you?"

**Final Question:**
50. "Is there anything else about quality management that I haven't asked about, but you think is important for designing these modules?"

**Thank You:**
"Your insights are incredibly valuable and will directly shape how we build these TQM modules. Thank you for your time and expertise!"

## Workflow Shadowing Observation Guide

### **Preparation**

**Pre-Visit:**
- Schedule 2-4 hour block when quality manager has typical tasks (not meetings)
- Bring: Notebook, camera (for screenshots/whiteboards), recording device (if consented)
- Review interview notes from this participant to know what to watch for

**Introduction:**
"Thank you for letting me shadow you today. I'm here to observe your natural workflow - please just go about your normal tasks and I'll quietly take notes. Feel free to think aloud if you're comfortable, explaining what you're doing and why. If anything is confidential, just let me know and I won't record it. Sound good?"

---

### **Observation Focus Areas**

**1. Physical Environment:**
- Where does quality manager work? (Office? Shared space? On the move?)
- What tools are within arm's reach? (Computer, binders, forms, phone?)
- Are quality documents easily accessible or tucked away?
- Is the workspace organized or chaotic? (Not judging - understanding context)

**2. Technology Usage:**
- What systems/software are used? (Excel, Word, email, third-party tools?)
- How often does quality manager switch between systems?
- Any manual data entry or duplicate entry?
- Any system frustrations observed? (Slow loading, crashes, confusing interfaces?)

**3. Communication Patterns:**
- Who does quality manager interact with? (Staff, managers, residents, families, external agencies?)
- Mode of communication? (Face-to-face, phone, email, WhatsApp?)
- How are quality issues escalated or reported?
- Any communication breakdowns observed?

**4. Task Workflows:**
- **If conducting an audit:** Watch the entire process
  - How is checklist accessed? (Print from computer? Pre-printed binder? Digital tablet?)
  - How are findings recorded? (Paper â†’ later transcribed? Direct entry?)
  - How long does audit take?
  - What happens immediately after audit? (Email report? File in cabinet?)
- **If investigating an incident:** Observe documentation process
  - Forms used? (Paper vs. digital?)
  - Who is interviewed? (How long does gathering info take?)
  - Where is incident logged?
  - Follow-up actions assigned how?
- **If preparing for meeting/inspection:** Watch evidence gathering
  - Where is evidence located? (Multiple folders? Different systems?)
  - How long to find specific data point?
  - Any evidence gaps or "I know I have that somewhere..."?

**5. Workarounds & Hacks:**
- Watch for unofficial processes (sticky notes, personal trackers)
- Listen for phrases like "I usually just..." or "The system doesn't do X, so I..."
- Note any manual processes that could be automated
- Identify double-entry (same data entered multiple places)

**6. Emotional Cues:**
- When does quality manager seem stressed? (Specific tasks?)
- When does quality manager seem satisfied/proud? (Completed task? Good finding?)
- Any visible frustration with current processes?

**Activities:**

- Quick round-robin: "Share one thing that frustrates you most about current quality management"
- Review workshop objectives and agenda
- Set ground rules (all ideas welcome, build on each other's ideas, no idea is too wild)

**Deliverable:** Engaged participants, shared understanding of goals

---

**9:15-10:15 AM: Feature Prioritization Exercise (60 min)**

**Activity 1: Feature Brainstorm (20 min)**
- Present findings from interviews/shadowing on large board
- Organized by TQM module:
  - **Quality Audits:** Template library, mobile checklist, photo documentation, action tracking, PDSA link, report auto-generation
  - **Incident Management:** Structured investigation, RCA tools (5 Whys, Fishbone), SPSP alignment, trend analysis, Duty of Candour tracking
  - **Feedback & Complaints:** Survey builder, suggestion box, complaint workflow, closed-loop tracking ("You said, we did"), resident QI involvement
  - **Training & Competency:** Competency assessments, skills matrix, needs analysis, link to quality issues
  - **Document Control:** Version control, approval workflows, read receipts, automatic review alerts
- Ask: "Any features I missed that you need?"
- Add new features on sticky notes

**Activity 2: MoSCoW Voting (20 min)**
- Explain MoSCoW method:
  - **Must Have:** System is not viable without this feature (MVP)
  - **Should Have:** Important but system can launch without it (next release)
  - **Could Have:** Nice to have if time/budget allows (future enhancement)
  - **Won't Have:** Not doing this (at least not now)
- Each participant gets 4 color-coded dot stickers per category
- Vote on each feature by placing dot
- Tally votes and discuss divergences

**Activity 3: Prioritization Discussion (20 min)**
- Review voting results
- Discuss features with split votes (some Must, some Could)
- Facilitator asks probing questions:
  - "For those who voted Must, why is this critical?"
  - "For those who voted Could, what concerns do you have?"
  - "If we could only deliver 5 features in the first release, which 5?"
- Converge on consensus prioritization

**Deliverable:** Prioritized feature list with Must/Should/Could/Won't classifications

#### **4. Non-Functional Requirements**

**NFR1: Performance**
- Audit report generation: <5 seconds for standard audit
- Mobile checklist loading: <2 seconds on 4G connection
- Support 50 concurrent auditors across 5 homes without degradation

**NFR2: Usability**
- Quality manager can complete first audit within 10 minutes of training (measured in UAT)
- Mobile interface optimized for one-handed use (thumbs-friendly targets)
- Accessibility: WCAG 2.1 AA compliance

**NFR3: Security**
- Role-based access: Only assigned auditor + quality manager can edit audit
- Audit trail: All changes logged (who, when, what changed)
- Resident photos: Blurred faces unless consent obtained

**NFR4: Compliance**
- GDPR: Audit data retained for 5 years, then auto-archived
- Care Inspectorate: Audit reports exportable in PDF format with digital signature
- HIS QMS: PDSA cycle linkage (audit findings trigger improvement projects)

#### **5. Integration Points**

**Staff Rota System:**
- Auditor assignment pulls from staff roster (only qualified staff appear in dropdown)
- Audit time blocked in schedule (prevents double-booking auditor)
- Training records linked (e.g., only Infection Control trained staff can conduct IC audits)

**Evidence Repository:**
- Completed audit reports auto-filed in appropriate quality theme folder
- Audit findings populate Evidence Index Master
- Photos stored with metadata (date, auditor, location, tags)

**Incident Management Module:**
- Audit non-compliances can trigger incident investigations
- Incident trends inform audit frequency (if falls increase, trigger falls prevention audits)

## Risk Mitigation

**Risk 1: Low Participant Recruitment**
- **Mitigation:** Offer flexibility in participation (virtual interviews if on-site difficult)
- **Mitigation:** Emphasize personal benefit (features designed for their needs)
- **Mitigation:** HOS endorsement email emphasizing importance

**Risk 2: Workshop Scheduling Conflicts**
- **Mitigation:** Send save-the-date 2 weeks in advance
- **Mitigation:** Offer alternative workshop date if 50%+ cannot attend primary date
- **Mitigation:** Hybrid workshop (in-person + video call for remote participants)

**Risk 3: Participant Fatigue (Too Much Involvement)**
- **Mitigation:** Clear time commitments upfront (total 7-10 hours over 4 weeks)
- **Mitigation:** Respect participant time (start/end on schedule)
- **Mitigation:** Show appreciation (thank you notes, recognition in documentation)

**Risk 4: Divergent Feature Priorities**
- **Mitigation:** Data-driven prioritization (MoSCoW voting, not just loudest voice)
- **Mitigation:** Focus on MVP consensus (can't please everyone in first release)
- **Mitigation:** Document all requests, commit to revisiting in future releases

**Risk 5: Unrealistic Feature Expectations**
- **Mitigation:** Technical feasibility assessment in parallel with co-design
- **Mitigation:** Transparent communication about constraints (time, budget, complexity)
- **Mitigation:** Frame as iterative (MVP now, enhancements later)
