## SAGE Harvard Citation Style Guidelines

### Journal Articles
**Format:** Author(s) (Year) Title of article. *Journal Name* Volume(Issue): page range.

**Example:**  
Burke EK, De Causmaecker P, Berghe GV and Van Landeghem H (2004) The state of the art of nurse rostering. *Journal of Scheduling* 7(6): 441-499.

### Books
**Format:** Author(s) (Year) *Book Title*. Edition (if not 1st). Place: Publisher.

**Example:**  
Forcier J, Bissex P and Chun WJ (2008) *Python Web Development with Django*. Upper Saddle River, NJ: Addison-Wesley.

### Book Chapters
**Format:** Author(s) (Year) Chapter title. In: Editor(s) (ed.) *Book Title*. Place: Publisher, pp. page range.

### Reports/Government Documents
**Format:** Organization (Year) *Report Title*. Report number (if applicable). Place: Publisher. Available at: URL (accessed date).

**Example:**  
Scottish Government (2025) *Digital Strategy for Scotland: Sustainable Digital Public Services Delivery Plan 2025-2028*. Edinburgh: Scottish Government. Available at: https://www.gov.scot/publications/digital-strategy-scotland-sustainable-digital-public-services-delivery-plan-2025-2028/ (accessed 20 December 2025).

### Websites
**Format:** Author/Organization (Year) Title. Available at: URL (accessed date).

## In-Text Citation Rules (SAGE Harvard)

### Single Author
First citation: (Brooke, 1996)  
Subsequent: (Brooke, 1996)

### Two Authors
(Burke and De Causmaecker, 2004)

### Three or More Authors
First citation: (Burke, De Causmaecker, Berghe and Van Landeghem, 2004) OR (Burke et al., 2004) - journal preference  
Subsequent: (Burke et al., 2004)

### Multiple Citations
(Burke et al., 2004; Cheang et al., 2003; Ernst et al., 2004) - chronological order

### Secondary Citations
If citing work mentioned in another source: (Smith, 1990, cited in Jones, 2020)  
Only Jones 2020 appears in reference list

### Page Numbers (for direct quotes)
(Burke et al., 2004: 445) or (Burke et al., 2004, p. 445)
