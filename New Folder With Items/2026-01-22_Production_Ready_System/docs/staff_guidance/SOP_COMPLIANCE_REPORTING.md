## Purpose

This SOP defines procedures for generating, reviewing, and using compliance reports to meet Care Inspectorate requirements, internal audits, and management reporting needs.

## Scope

- Daily operational reports
- Weekly management summaries
- Monthly compliance reporting
- Quarterly trend analysis
- Annual audit packs
- Care Inspectorate inspection preparation

## Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| **Unit Manager** | Generate daily reports, review unit compliance, action issues |
| **Compliance Officer** | Generate monthly reports, monitor trends, prepare for inspections |
| **Senior Manager** | Review quarterly reports, strategic planning, sign off compliance |
| **Care Inspectorate Liaison** | Annual audit packs, inspection responses, improvement plans |

---

## Report Categories

### 1. Care Plan Review Compliance

**What It Shows:**
- Total residents (120 across 8 units)
- Reviews due this month
- Reviews completed on time
- Reviews overdue (>0 days past due)
- Average completion time
- Breakdown by unit
- Breakdown by review type (INITIAL 4-week, SIX_MONTH, UNSCHEDULED)

**Who Uses It:** Unit Managers (daily), Senior Managers (monthly), Care Inspectorate (annual)

**Required Frequency:** Weekly minimum, daily recommended

---

### 2. Training & Qualifications Matrix

**What It Shows:**
- All staff training records
- Mandatory training compliance (e.g., Moving & Handling, Fire Safety)
- Qualifications held (SVQ 2, SVQ 3, RN, etc.)
- Training renewals due (next 30/60/90 days)
- Expired training (requires immediate action)
- Induction completion for new staff
- Supervision records (1-2-1 meetings)

**Who Uses It:** Unit Managers (monthly), HR (recruitment), Care Inspectorate (inspection)

**Required Frequency:** Monthly

---

### 3. Annual Leave & Absence

**What It Shows:**
- Leave balances by staff
- Leave usage % vs. target (quarterly)
- Carry-over summary
- Sickness absence rates
- Bradford Factor scores (sickness pattern analysis)
- Return-to-work interview completion

**Who Uses It:** Unit Managers (weekly), HR (absence management), Finance (leave liability)

**Required Frequency:** Weekly for absence, monthly for leave compliance

---

### 4. Additional Staffing Costs

**What It Shows:**
- Overtime hours and cost (by unit, by staff, by month)
- Agency usage and cost (by unit, by agency, by month)
- Budget vs. actual variance
- Trends over time
- Cost per resident day
- Recommendations for cost control

**Who Uses It:** Unit Managers (weekly), Finance (budget monitoring), Senior Managers (strategic planning)

**Required Frequency:** Weekly (auto-generated Monday 8 AM)

---

### 5. Staffing Levels & Skill Mix

**What It Shows:**
- Daily staffing levels vs. minimum requirements
- Percentage of shifts meeting minimum staffing
- Skill mix per shift (senior carer, care staff, ratio)
- Staff-to-resident ratios
- Dependency levels by unit
- Gaps/shortfalls

**Who Uses It:** Unit Managers (daily), Operations (planning), Care Inspectorate (safe staffing)

**Required Frequency:** Daily

---

### 6. Incident & Safeguarding

**What It Shows:**
- Incidents recorded (falls, medication errors, behavior, etc.)
- Severity levels (low, medium, high, critical)
- Actions taken
- Safeguarding concerns raised
- Safeguarding investigations outcomes
- Trends and patterns

**Who Uses It:** Unit Managers (immediate), Safeguarding Lead (weekly), Care Inspectorate (as required)

**Required Frequency:** Immediate for serious incidents, weekly summary

---

### 7. DBS & Registration Compliance

**What It Shows:**
- All staff DBS checks status (valid, expiring, expired)
- DBS renewals due (next 30/60/90 days)
- Professional registration (SSSC for care staff, NMC for nurses)
- Registration renewals due
- Staff working without valid checks (critical issue)

**Who Uses It:** HR (monthly), Compliance Officer (monthly), Care Inspectorate (inspection)

**Required Frequency:** Monthly minimum, weekly recommended

---

### 8. Supervision & Appraisal

**What It Shows:**
- Supervision records (1-2-1 meetings with staff)
- Frequency (target: every 6 weeks)
- Overdue supervisions
- Annual appraisal completion
- Performance issues identified
- Development plans

**Who Uses It:** Unit Managers (ongoing), HR (performance management), Care Inspectorate (staff development)

**Required Frequency:** Monthly

## Procedure: Preparing for Care Inspectorate Inspection

### When Inspection Announced

**Typical Notice:** 2-6 weeks advance notice (or unannounced)

---

### Step 1: Generate Inspection Pack (Day 1)

1. **Navigate to:** Reports ‚Üí Care Inspectorate ‚Üí Inspection Pack
2. **Select Date Range:** Last 12 months
3. **Click "Generate Full Inspection Pack"**

**Processing Time:** 2-3 minutes

**Pack Includes:**

- Care plan review compliance (12-month summary)
- Training matrix (all staff, all courses)
- DBS and registration checks
- Staffing levels analysis
- Incident summary
- Safeguarding records
- Complaints and compliments
- Quality assurance audits
- Policies and procedures (latest versions)
- Improvement plans from previous inspection

**Format:** Combined PDF (100-150 pages typically)

---

### Step 2: Review Pack for Issues

**Check Each Section:**

1. **Care Plan Reviews:**
   - Any currently overdue? ‚Üí Complete immediately
   - Overall compliance ‚â•95%? ‚Üí If not, explain why

2. **Training:**
   - Any expired? ‚Üí Urgent renewal
   - Overall ‚â•95%? ‚Üí Book courses for missing staff

3. **DBS/Registration:**
   - Any lapsed? ‚Üí Staff cannot work
   - Any renewals overdue? ‚Üí Chase immediately

4. **Staffing:**
   - Any shifts below minimum? ‚Üí Must have documented reason (emergency)
   - Skill mix adequate? ‚Üí Check senior carer on each shift

5. **Incidents:**
   - All investigated? ‚Üí Complete any outstanding
   - Patterns identified? ‚Üí Document actions taken

6. **Safeguarding:**
   - All concerns raised appropriately? ‚Üí Check
   - All investigations completed? ‚Üí Check outcomes documented

---

### Step 3: Fix Critical Issues (Week 1-2)

**Priority Actions:**

**Red (Must Fix Before Inspection):**
- Complete all overdue care plan reviews
- Renew all expired DBS/training
- Complete all outstanding incident investigations
- Ensure all safeguarding concerns documented

**Yellow (Should Fix):**
- Improve compliance % to ‚â•95%
- Schedule missing supervisions
- Update any outdated policies

**Green (Nice to Have):**
- Tidy up records
- Ensure consistency of documentation
- Prepare staff for potential interviews

---

### Step 4: Prepare Staff (Week 2-3)

**Brief All Staff:**

"Care Inspectorate inspection on [date]. What to expect:"

1. **Inspectors May:**
   - Observe your work
   - Ask about your role, training, supervision
   - Ask to see records (care plans, rotas, etc.)
   - Speak to residents and families

2. **What Staff Should Know:**
   - Where to find policies
   - Who their supervisor is
   - When their last training was
   - How to raise safeguarding concerns
   - How to access the rota

3. **Be Honest:**
   - If you don't know something, say so
   - Offer to find out or direct to manager
   - Don't guess or make things up

4. **Show Our Strengths:**
   - Person-centered care
   - Strong teamwork
   - Good training
   - Open communication

---

### Step 5: Prepare Physical Environment (Week 3-4)

**Check:**

- Notice boards up to date (policies, contact numbers)
- Staff room tidy (rotas visible, guidance available)
- Care plans accessible (locked but organized)
- Medication storage secure and correct
- Health & safety checks up to date (fire alarms, water temps, etc.)
- Residents' rooms respectful (privacy, dignity)

---

### Step 6: On Inspection Day

**Morning of Inspection:**

1. **Generate Today's Daily Report:** Show current status
2. **Have Inspection Pack Ready:** Physical copy and USB
3. **Identify Point of Contact:** Usually manager or senior person
4. **Brief On-Duty Staff:** Inspectors here today, be helpful
5. **Notify Families:** Inspectors may speak to them

**During Inspection:**

- Be available to answer questions
- Provide documents promptly
- Don't hide issues - explain actions taken
- Show evidence of continuous improvement

**After Inspection:**

- Note any areas inspectors focused on
- Identify likely areas of concern
- Start preparing action plan for any issues
- Brief staff on how it went

---

### Step 7: Await Report (4-6 Weeks Later)

**When Report Received:**

1. **Review Grades:**
   - Key question ratings (1-6 scale)
   - Requirements (must fix)
   - Recommendations (should consider)

2. **Create Action Plan:**
   - Address all requirements (legal obligation)
   - Consider all recommendations
   - Set timelines
   - Assign responsibilities

3. **Submit Action Plan:** Usually within 14 days

4. **Implement Improvements:** Track using system action tracker

5. **Follow-Up Inspection:** If required, prepare again

## Troubleshooting

### Issue: Report shows incorrect data

**Checks:**

1. **Date range correct?** Check you selected the right period
2. **Filters applied?** Check unit/staff filters
3. **Data entered correctly?** Check source records
4. **Report refreshed?** Try regenerating

**If Still Wrong:**
- Check data in system (e.g., care plan records)
- Correct source data
- Regenerate report
- If persists, contact system support

---

### Issue: Report won't generate

**Common Causes:**

1. **Too much data:** Try smaller date range
2. **System busy:** Try again in a few minutes
3. **Permission issue:** Check you have access rights
4. **Technical error:** Check error message, contact support

---

### Issue: Report missing expected data

**Checks:**

1. **Was data entered?** Check system for source records
2. **Date range include data?** Expand date range to check
3. **Filter hiding data?** Remove filters and try again
