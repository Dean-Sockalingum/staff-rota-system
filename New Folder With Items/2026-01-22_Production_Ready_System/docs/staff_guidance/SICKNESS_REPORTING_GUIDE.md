## Reporting Sickness Absence

### Before Your Shift Starts

**Step 1: Call Your Line Manager**

**Why phone, not text?**
- Manager needs to ask follow-up questions
- They need to assess severity
- Texts can be missed
- It's company policy (contractual requirement)

**When to call:**
- **Day shifts (07:00 start):** Call by 06:45 latest
- **Night shifts (19:00 start):** Call by 18:45 latest
- **Earlierthe better** - gives time to arrange cover

**Can't reach manager?**
- Try 3 times
- Call main office/duty manager
- Leave voicemail AND call office
- Send text ONLY as backup (not replacement)

**Step 2: Provide Key Information**

Your manager will ask:

1. **Nature of illness**
   - You don't need to give full medical history
   - General description is enough ("stomach bug", "flu symptoms")
   - Be honest - helps with risk assessment

2. **Symptoms**
   - Relevant for infection control
   - Determines if you can return
   - May need specific clearance (48hr rule for D&V)

3. **Expected return**
   - Best estimate
   - OK to say "unsure"
   - Can update daily if needed

4. **GP consulted?**
   - Have you seen/will you see a doctor?
   - Any medication prescribed?
   - Any fit note issued?

**Step 3: Understand Next Steps**

Manager will confirm:
- Your absence is recorded
- Cover arrangements (not your problem!)
- When to call back
- Certification requirements
- Any actions you need to take

### During Your Shift

**If You Become Unwell At Work:**

1. **Tell shift coordinator immediately**
   - Don't struggle on
   - Don't just disappear
   - Patient safety priority

2. **Complete incident form**
   - Especially if injury or exposure
   - Occupational health may be involved

3. **Get permission to leave**
   - Don't leave site without approval
   - Handover your duties
   - Sign out properly

4. **Follow up with phone call**
   - Call your line manager later
   - Confirm you're off sick tomorrow (if applicable)
   - Follow normal sickness procedures

## Certification Requirements

### Self-Certification (Days 1-7)

**What is it?**
- You declare your illness yourself
- No doctor's note needed
- Available for up to 7 calendar days

**How to do it:**

1. **Log into the system:**
   - Go to http://localhost:8000/
   - Click **Report Sickness** or **My Absences**

2. **Complete the form:**
   - Date sickness started
   - Nature of illness (dropdown + description)
   - Expected return date
   - Confirm you called your manager

3. **Submit:**
   - Form goes to manager
   - They may add notes from phone conversation
   - Recorded in your absence history

**Rules:**
- Must be submitted within 48 hours of return
- Can be completed while off sick or on return
- Must be truthful and accurate
- Manager may ask follow-up questions

### Medical Certification (Day 8+)

**GP Fit Note Required:**

If you're off for 8 **calendar days** or more (including weekends):

1. **Visit your GP:**
   - Book appointment as soon as you know you'll be off 8+ days
   - Explain your job (care worker, 12-hour shifts, etc.)
   - Get a fit note (free for employees)

2. **Fit Note Options:**
   - **Not fit for work** - Cannot work at all
   - **May be fit for work with adjustments** - Phased return possible

3. **Submit to employer:**
   - Scan/photo and email to your manager
   - Or post original (keep a copy!)
   - **Within 48 hours** of obtaining it
   - System will prompt you if overdue

**Ongoing Certification:**

- Fit notes issued for specific periods (e.g., 2 weeks)
- You must get a new fit note before current one expires
- No gap allowed - see GP before current note ends
- Continuous certification = continuous sick pay

## Return to Work

### The Return-to-Work Interview

**What is it?**
- Informal, supportive meeting with your line manager
- Mandatory after every sickness absence
- Usually 10-15 minutes
- Conducted within 48 hours of return

**Purpose:**
- ‚úÖ Welcome you back
- ‚úÖ Check you're fit to return
- ‚úÖ Discuss any support needed
- ‚úÖ Update records
- ‚úÖ Identify any concerns
- ‚ùå NOT disciplinary (unless misconduct suspected)

**What to expect:**

1. **How you're feeling**
   - Manager checks you're genuinely recovered
   - Whether you need any adjustments

2. **About your absence**
   - Confirm dates and reason
   - Check if work-related
   - Discuss any occupational health referral

3. **Pattern discussion (if applicable)**
   - If frequent or concerning pattern
   - Not judgmental - supportive
   - Identify if help needed

4. **Paperwork**
   - Sign return-to-work form
   - Confirm self-certification submitted
   - Check any fit notes recorded

**Your rights:**
- Meeting should be private
- You can have a colleague present (if you want)
- Notes kept confidential
- Any concerns addressed

### Phased Return

**What is it?**
- Gradual return to full hours/duties
- After long-term sickness
- Supported by fit note or occupational health

**Example:**
- Week 1: 2 shifts (50% hours)
- Week 2: 3 shifts (75% hours)
- Week 3: Full rota (100%)

**How to arrange:**
- Discuss with manager before return
- May need OH recommendation
- Paid at reduced hours or full pay (depends on policy)
- Review progress weekly

## Absence Triggers & Bradford Factor

### What is the Bradford Factor?

**The Bradford Factor** is a formula that measures the **disruption** of absence, not just the time off.

**Formula:** B = S¬≤ √ó D
- **S** = Number of absence spells (periods)
- **D** = Total days absent
- **B** = Bradford Factor score

**Why it matters:**
- Frequent short absences cause more disruption than one long absence
- Used to identify patterns needing support
- Triggers management review, not automatic discipline

**Example:**

**Person A:**
- 1 absence of 10 days
- Bradford = 1¬≤ √ó 10 = **10**

**Person B:**
- 10 absences of 1 day each
- Bradford = 10¬≤ √ó 10 = **1000**

Person B has same time off but 100√ó the Bradford score because frequency disrupts team more.

### Trigger Points

**When Bradford Factor reaches certain levels, triggers apply:**

| Score | Action |
|-------|--------|
| **0-50** | No action - normal monitoring |
| **51-200** | **First Review** - Informal chat with manager |
| **201-400** | **Second Review** - Formal review meeting |
| **401-600** | **Third Review** - Written warning possible |
| **601+** | **Fourth Review** - Further disciplinary action |

**Reviews are supportive, not punitive:**
- Identify underlying causes
- Offer support (OH referral, EAP, adjustments)
- Check if work-related issues
- Only disciplinary if no genuine illness

### Absence Triggers

**Separate from Bradford Factor:**

**Trigger 1: Frequency**
- **4 or more absences in 12 months**
- Triggers review meeting

**Trigger 2: Duration**
- **10+ days in 12-month period**
- Triggers review meeting

**Trigger 3: Patterns**
- Absences before/after leave
- Repeated Mondays or Fridays
- After shift change requests denied
- Triggers investigation

**What Happens:**
1. Manager invites you to meeting
2. Discuss absence pattern
3. Identify support needed
4. May refer to OH
5. Agree action plan
6. Monitor improvement

**Protected Absences (Don't Count):**
- Pregnancy-related sickness
- Disability-related (with medical evidence)
- Injury at work (IOD)
- COVID-19 (during pandemic period)
- Some long-term conditions (case by case)

## Types of Absence

### Sickness Absence
- Illness or injury preventing work
- Certificated as required
- Sick pay applies (if eligible)

### Infection Control Exclusions

**48-Hour Rule (Diarrhea & Vomiting):**
- Must be symptom-free for 48 hours before return
- Applies even if you feel better
- Infection control requirement (Care Inspectorate)
- Full pay applies

**Other Exclusions:**
- COVID-19: Follow current NHS Scotland guidance
- Infectious diseases: Await clearance
- Skin infections: Covered, clearance needed

### Work-Related Injury

**Injury On Duty (IOD):**
- Injury sustained at work
- Report via incident form immediately
- May trigger HSE notification
- Different sick pay rules
- OH assessment required

### Pregnancy-Related

- Antenatal appointments (paid time off)
- Pregnancy-related illness (doesn't count for triggers)
- Discuss maternity leave planning early

### Stress/Mental Health

- Treated same as physical illness
- Certification required (GP or counselor)
- OH referral for support
- Risk assessment on return
- Adjustments considered

## Sick Pay Entitlement

### Statutory Sick Pay (SSP)

**Basic Entitlement:**
- ¬£109.40 per week (2025 rate)
- Paid for up to 28 weeks
- Qualifying conditions:
  - Earn at least ¬£123/week
  - Off sick 4+ consecutive days
  - Properly certified

### Occupational Sick Pay

**Enhanced Scheme (After 6 months service):**

| Service | Full Pay | Half Pay |
|---------|----------|----------|
| **6-12 months** | 1 month | 1 month |
| **1-2 years** | 2 months | 2 months |
| **2-3 years** | 3 months | 3 months |
| **3-5 years** | 4 months | 4 months |
| **5+ years** | 5 months | 5 months |

**After occupational pay ends:**
- Revert to SSP
- Up to 28 weeks total

**Notes:**
- 12-month rolling period
- Includes any previous sickness
- Manager can extend (discretionary)
- Final decision: HR Director

### During Probation

- SSP only (no occupational sick pay)
- Unless exceptional circumstances
- Excessive sickness may extend/terminate probation

## Support Available

### Occupational Health (OH)

**What is OH?**
- Independent healthcare professionals
- Advise on fitness for work
- Recommend adjustments
- Monitor work-related health

**When referred:**
- Long-term sickness (4+ weeks)
- Frequent short-term absence
- Work-related stress
- Disability adjustments needed
- Pregnancy complications
- Return to work support

**The process:**
1. Manager refers you (with your consent)
2. OH contacts you for appointment
3. Assessment (phone or face-to-face)
4. Report to manager (with your consent)
5. Recommendations implemented

**Your rights:**
- You can request OH referral
- Appointments are confidential
- You see the report before manager
- You can challenge recommendations

### Employee Assistance Programme (EAP)

**Free Confidential Support:**
- 24/7 helpline: [Number]
- Phone or face-to-face counseling
- Legal/financial advice
- Bereavement support
- Stress management

**You DON'T need:**
- Manager's permission
- To tell anyone you've called
- To be off sick

**Services:**
- Up to 6 counseling sessions
- Self-referral
- Family members can use it too

### Trade Union

- Advice on rights
- Accompaniment to meetings
- Representation in disputes
- Check policies

## Disciplinary Process

### When Does Sickness Become Disciplinary?

**Genuine sickness is NOT a disciplinary matter.**

**Disciplinary action only if:**
- ‚ùå False sickness claims
- ‚ùå Working elsewhere while off sick
- ‚ùå Activities inconsistent with illness
- ‚ùå Refusing to engage with support
- ‚ùå Refusing OH referral
- ‚ùå Failing to certificate properly
- ‚ùå Failure to keep in touch

### Capability Procedure

**Different from discipline:**
- Used when genuinely unable to work
- After all support tried
- OH confirms no reasonable adjustments
- Last resort: ill-health retirement or termination

**Process:**
1. Multiple OH assessments
2. Trial of adjustments
3. Redeployment considered
4. Formal capability hearing
5. Appeal rights

## Special Circumstances

### Cosmetic Surgery

- Unpaid leave only
- Must use annual leave
- No sick pay
- Exceptions: Reconstructive (medical)

### Caring for Dependents

**Emergency Time Off:**
- Unpaid statutory right
- Short-term emergency only (e.g., child's sudden illness)
- Reasonable time to arrange care
- Not for planned caring

**Options:**
- Annual leave
- Parental leave
- Flexible working request
- Unpaid leave (discretionary)

### Dental/Medical Appointments

- Use annual leave
- Or unpaid time off
- Emergency appointments: Manager discretion
- Chronic conditions: Reasonable adjustments

## FAQs

### Q: Can I be sacked for being off sick?

**A:** Not for genuine illness. However:
- During probation: Higher risk
- Persistent absence despite support: Capability process
- Fake sickness: Gross misconduct

### Q: What if I disagree with OH report?

**A:** You can:
- Get second opinion (your cost)
- Provide GP letter
- Formal challenge to OH
- Grievance if treated unfairly

### Q: Can I go on holiday while off sick?

**A:** Depends:
- If doctor says travel is therapeutic: YES (with fit note)
- If you're too sick to work: Usually NO
- Check with manager first
- Can look like misconduct if not approved

### Q: What if I'm sick on annual leave?

**A:** If you have GP fit note:
- Annual leave converted to sick leave
- Leave days credited back
- Must call manager immediately
- Certificate required

### Q: Do I have to answer health questions?

**A:** 
- General nature of illness: YES
- Detailed medical info: NO (only to OH)
- Balance: Enough for cover arrangements

### Q: Can I be dismissed during sick leave?

**A:** Yes, but only for:
- Conduct issues (unrelated to sickness)
- Redundancy
- Capability (after full process)
- Gross misconduct
