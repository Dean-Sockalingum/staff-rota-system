Introduction  
This checklist  sets out what you’ll need to do both before and during the attendance review 
meeting.   This meeting is required with the employee  where their absence record breaches the 
Council’s trigger points.  You can find more information on this in  supporting attendance: 
manager's guide.       
 
 
Before the meeting:                                                                                                                    (✓) 
• Confirm the arrang ements for the attendance review meeting . This includes  
advising the employee of their right to be accompanied by a colleague or trade 
union represen tative  (it’s the employee’s responsibility to arrange this).  
 □ 
• Arran ge for the interview to take place in a private setting.  □) 
• Allow for enough time for all the issues to be discussed.   □ 
 
At the meeting:                                                                                                                            (✓) 
• Confirm the trigger point breached.  □ 
• Explore the reasons for their absence and their overall level of attendance.  □ 
• Explain the impact of their absence on service delivery, other colleagues and 
workload levels.  
 □ 
• Listen to and consider th e employee’s responses.  □ 
• Identify any areas of support and concern.  □ 
• If appropriate, consider a referral to the Occupational Health Service (OHS) or 
Employee Assistance Programme (EAP)  
 □ 
• Consider whether disciplinary action is appropriate (this will normally be in cases of 
misconduct where the employee has failed to follow absence reporting procedures 
with unauthorised absences ). 
 □ 
• Advise the employee that further absences may result in management action 
where their attendance is giving cause for co ncern and is reaching an 
unsustainable level.  
 □ 
• Complete the formal/follow up interview checklist , recording  any further action 
that you intend to take. This should be followed up in writing  
 □ 
• For cases of misconduct, arrange a disc iplinary interview in line  with the Council’s 
disciplinary procedures . 
 □
