# Annual Leave Usage Targets Guide

**For Managers: Preventing Year-End Leave Build-up**

---

## Quick Summary

To prevent staff from accumulating excessive leave and causing year-end disruption, managers should monitor leave usage against the **40-week target**:

- **Target**: Staff should use approximately **1 day of leave per week** over 40 working weeks
- **Simplified**: **4-5 days per month** consistently throughout the year
- **Result**: All annual leave used evenly, preventing rushes and ensuring adequate rest

---

## The 40-Week Strategy

### Why 40 Weeks?

- **52 weeks** in a year
- Minus **~8 weeks** for bank holidays, unit closures, training days
- Minus **~4 weeks** buffer for flexibility
- = **40 active working weeks** for planned leave usage

### Target by Entitlement Level

| Entitlement | Days/Week Target | Days/Month Target |
|-------------|------------------|-------------------|
| 40 days (full-time) | ~1.0 day | ~4.3 days |
| 35 days (part-time) | ~0.9 day | ~3.8 days |
| 30 days (reduced) | ~0.8 day | ~3.3 days |
| 45 days (enhanced) | ~1.1 days | ~4.9 days |

## Monthly Checkpoints

### By End of March (Q1)
- **Expected**: 33% of leave used (~13 days for full-time)
- **Red flag**: Less than 9 days used

### By End of June (Q2)  
- **Expected**: 65% of leave used (~26 days for full-time)
- **Red flag**: Less than 19 days used
- **Action**: If red, require immediate leave booking

### By End of September (Q3)
- **Expected**: 98% of leave used (~39 days for full-time)
- **Red flag**: Less than 30 days used
- **Action**: Enforce mandatory leave before year-end

## Common Issues & Solutions

### "I'm too busy to take leave"
**Response**: Taking leave is mandatory, not optional. Book at least 1 day per fortnight.

### "I'm saving it for later"
**Risk**: Year-end rush, service disruption, lost leave
**Response**: Explain 40-week strategy, set interim quarterly targets

### Coverage concerns
**Solution**: Stagger leave, plan 4 weeks ahead, use rota system

### Excessive carryover requests
**Prevention**: Monitor quarterly, enforce targets, limit carryover
