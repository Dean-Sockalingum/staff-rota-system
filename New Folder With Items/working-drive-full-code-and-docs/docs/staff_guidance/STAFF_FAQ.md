## Annual Leave

### How do I request annual leave?

**Step-by-Step Guide:**

1. **Check Your Leave Entitlement**
   - Log in to the Staff Rota System at http://localhost:8000/
   - Navigate to **My Profile** â†’ **Leave Balance**
   - View your total entitlement, used days, and remaining balance

2. **Submit Your Request**
   - Click **Request Leave** from the main menu
   - Select the dates you wish to take off (start and end date)
   - Add any notes explaining your request (optional)
   - Click **Submit Request**

3. **What Happens Next?**
   - Your line manager will receive an email notification
   - They will review staffing levels for your requested dates
   - You'll receive an email when your request is approved or denied
   - Status updates appear in **My Leave Requests**

4. **Notice Periods**
   - **Standard requests:** Minimum 14 days notice
   - **Emergency/compassionate leave:** Contact your manager directly
   - **Peak periods (Christmas, Easter):** Submit requests at least 6 weeks in advance

5. **Important Rules**
   - Maximum 20% of team can be on leave simultaneously
   - Leave requests are approved on a first-come, first-served basis
   - Once approved, changes require manager approval
   - Unused leave may be carried over (check your contract)

### What if my leave request is denied?

- You'll receive an email explaining the reason (usually staffing shortfall)
- Contact your manager to discuss alternative dates
- Emergency situations may override normal rules - speak with your manager

### How much annual leave am I entitled to?

**Full-Time Staff:**
- Minimum: 5.6 weeks (28 days) per year
- This includes bank holidays

**Part-Time Staff:**
- Pro-rata calculation based on contracted hours
- Example: If you work 3 days/week, you get 16.8 days (3/5 Ã— 28)

**View your exact entitlement in your staff profile**

## Sickness Reporting & Policy

### What do I do if I'm sick and can't come to work?

**STEP 1: Report Your Absence (Same Day)**

**Before Your Shift Starts:**
- **Call your manager directly** - Do NOT text or email only
- **Call at least 2 hours before your shift** (earlier for opening shifts)
- **Speak to a person** - Voicemail is not sufficient for first notification

**What to Tell Your Manager:**
- Your name and staff ID (SAP number)
- Nature of illness (brief description)
- When symptoms started
- Expected return date (if known)
- Contact number where you can be reached

**Manager Contact Numbers:**
- **Day Shifts:** [Manager name] - [Phone number]
- **Night Shifts:** [Night Manager] - [Phone number]
- **Weekends:** On-call manager - [Phone number]

**STEP 2: Daily Updates**
- **Call each day you remain off sick**
- Update your manager on your condition
- Confirm if you can return the next day or if absence continues

**STEP 3: Medical Evidence**

| Absence Length | What You Need |
|----------------|---------------|
| **1-7 days** | Self-certification (complete return-to-work form when you return) |
| **8+ days** | GP Fit Note (Statement of Fitness for Work) required |
| **Ongoing** | New fit notes required when previous one expires |

### Where do I submit my fit note?

**Option 1: In Person**
- Hand to your line manager on return to work
- They will scan and upload to your staff record

**Option 2: Email**
- Scan or photograph your fit note
- Email to: [HR email address]
- Subject line: "Fit Note - [Your Name] - [Staff ID]"

**Option 3: Post**
- Send to: [HR postal address]
- Mark envelope: "CONFIDENTIAL - Fit Note"

### What happens when I return to work?

**Return-to-Work Meeting (Mandatory)**

Within **48 hours** of your return, you'll have a meeting with your manager to:

1. **Welcome you back**
2. **Complete a Return-to-Work Form** covering:
   - Reason for absence
   - Whether you saw a doctor
   - If you're fully fit or need adjustments
   - Any workplace issues that contributed

3. **Discuss any support needed:**
   - Phased return (shorter shifts initially)
   - Altered duties temporarily
   - Occupational Health referral
   - Workplace adjustments

4. **Review attendance record:**
   - Your manager will review your absence history
   - Identify any patterns or concerns
   - Offer support if needed

### What if I'm sick multiple times?

**Trigger Points for Formal Review:**

The system automatically flags:
- **3 or more absences** in a 3-month period
- **10 or more working days** lost in a 12-month period
- **Patterns** (e.g., frequent Monday/Friday absences)

**What This Means:**
- Informal meeting with your manager
- Occupational Health referral may be offered
- Support plan developed
- Not disciplinary at this stage (unless evidence of abuse)

### What about stress-related absence?

**Immediate Actions:**
1. Contact your manager and mention it's stress-related
2. Manager will follow specific stress absence protocol
3. **Confidential support available:**
   - Employee Assistance Programme (EAP): [Phone number]
   - Occupational Health: [Contact details]
   - Trade Union representative

**You will NOT be pressured to return before you're ready**

## Shift Management

### How do I view my rota?

**Online Access:**
1. Log in at http://localhost:8000/
2. Click **My Rota** from the dashboard
3. View options:
   - **Weekly view:** Current week's shifts
   - **Monthly view:** Full month calendar
   - **Print version:** Printable PDF

**Shift Details Include:**
- Date and shift type (DAY/NIGHT)
- Unit assignment
- Start and end times
- Any special notes

### Can I swap shifts with a colleague?

**Yes - Follow This Process:**

1. **Find a Colleague to Swap With**
   - Must have same role/competency
   - Must maintain safe staffing levels
   - Both must agree to swap

2. **Submit Swap Request**
   - Go to **Shift Swaps** in the system
   - Select the shift you want to give away
   - Select the shift you'll take in return
   - Enter colleague's name/ID
   - Submit request

3. **Approval Process**
   - Both you and your colleague must confirm
   - Your manager reviews for compliance
   - You'll receive email notification of decision

4. **Rules**
   - Minimum **48 hours notice** before shift
   - Must not result in excessive hours
   - Cannot breach Working Time Directive rules

### What if I need emergency time off?

**Emergency Situations Include:**
- Family bereavement
- Dependent care crisis (child/elderly parent)
- Domestic emergency (flood, break-in)

**Immediate Actions:**
1. **Call your manager ASAP** - explain the situation
2. They will arrange emergency cover
3. You may use:
   - Emergency annual leave (if available)
   - Compassionate leave (unpaid/paid depending on policy)
   - Time off for dependents

**Do NOT just fail to show up** - this is considered absence without leave

## Manager Resources

### Managing Attendance (For Line Managers)

**Daily Tasks:**

1. **Review Absence Notifications**
   - Check voicemail and emails first thing
   - Log into Staff Rota System â†’ **Absence Dashboard**
   - Mark absences and arrange cover

2. **Conduct Return-to-Work Interviews**
   - Schedule within 48 hours of return
   - Use template form in system
   - Upload completed form to staff record

3. **Monitor Staffing Levels**
   - Dashboard shows current coverage
   - Alerts for understaffing
   - Compliance warnings for WTD breaches

**Using the Telephone Checklist:**

When staff call in sick, use the structured checklist:
- [See manager_telephone_checklist.md](/docs/staff_guidance/manager_telephone_checklist.md)

**Key Points:**
- âœ… How they're feeling and nature of illness
- âœ… Expected return date
- âœ… Whether GP consulted
- âœ… Any urgent work to reallocate
- âœ… Next contact date agreed
- âœ… Update system immediately after call

**Compliance Reports:**

Access compliance reports:
- **Violations Dashboard:** http://localhost:8000/scheduling/compliance/violations/
- **Weekly Summary:** Emailed every Monday 9am
- **Critical Alerts:** Real-time for serious issues

**Types of Violations to Monitor:**
- Understaffing (below minimum safe levels)
- Working Time Directive breaches (excessive hours)
- Skill mix ratio violations

### Leave Approval Guidelines

**Before Approving Leave:**

1. **Check Staffing Levels**
   - System shows projected coverage
   - Minimum safe levels must be maintained
   - 20% maximum team absence rule

2. **Consider:**
   - First-come, first-served (fair allocation)
   - Essential skills coverage (senior staff)
   - Pending leave requests from others
   - Resident care needs (high dependency periods)

3. **Deny Requests When:**
   - Below minimum staffing threshold
   - Too many from same team/unit already approved
   - Critical skills gap would result
   - Peak demand period (pre-Care Inspectorate inspection, Christmas)

**Communication:**
- Always explain reasons for denial
- Offer alternative dates
- Be consistent and fair

### Investigating Absence Patterns

**System Alerts for:**
- Bradford Factor score >200 (frequent short absences)
- 3+ absences in 3 months
- Monday/Friday pattern detected
- Post-night shift absences

**Investigation Process:**

1. **Review Data**
   - Print absence report from system
   - Look for patterns (dates, day of week, post-events)
   - Check return-to-work interview notes

2. **Informal Meeting**
   - Discuss concerns compassionately
   - Ask about underlying issues
   - Offer support (OH referral, EAP, adjustments)

3. **Support Plan**
   - Set review targets
   - Agree support measures
   - Document everything
   - Follow up regularly

4. **Formal Process (if no improvement)**
   - Consult HR before proceeding
   - Follow HSCP Supporting Attendance Policy
   - Trade union representative may attend
   - Potential outcomes: warnings, redeployment, dismissal (rare)

**Remember:** Most absence is genuine illness - approach with empathy

### Emergency Procedures

**Staff Member Doesn't Show Up (No Call):**

1. **Immediate Actions:**
   - Try calling/texting the staff member (3 attempts)
   - Check if they're on annual leave/forgotten shift
   - Arrange emergency cover from bank/agency
   - Log as "absent without leave" in system

2. **When Contact Made:**
   - Establish reason (genuine emergency vs. no-show)
   - Follow appropriate procedure (sickness vs. disciplinary)

3. **No Contact After 24 Hours:**
   - Escalate to senior management
   - Consider welfare check (especially if out of character)
   - HR involvement required

**Critical Staffing Shortfall:**

If unable to meet minimum safe staffing:
1. Call emergency bank staff immediately
2. Request voluntary overtime from off-duty staff
3. Escalate to senior management/on-call director
4. Document incident thoroughly
5. Complete incident report
6. **Never operate below safe levels**
