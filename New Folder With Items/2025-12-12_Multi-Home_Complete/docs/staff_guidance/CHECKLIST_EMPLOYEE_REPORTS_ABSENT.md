## Quick Reference: Contact Requirements

### What the Employee Must Do

| Timeline | Action Required |
|----------|----------------|
| **Day 1** | Contact you **immediately** by telephone to report absence |
| **Day 4** | Contact you if still absent |
| **Day 7** | Contact you if still absent + submit fit note if absence continues |
| **Day 14+** | Contact you every 7 days + submit ongoing fit notes |

### What You Should Do

‚úÖ **Immediately**
- Take the call or return the call as soon as possible
- Ask the questions above
- Document the conversation
- Make arrangements to cover their duties

‚úÖ **Consider**
- Wellbeing Support Meeting (if musculoskeletal/psychological condition)
- OH referral (if absence likely to be extended)
- EAP referral (if personal/stress issues)

‚úÖ **Follow Up**
- Ensure employee knows when to make next contact
- Monitor contact compliance
- Arrange return to work discussion when they return

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Employee Calls with Flu Symptoms

**Questions to emphasize:**
- Have they sought medical advice?
- How long do they expect to be absent?
- What action have they taken? (Rest, fluids, medication)
- When will they next make contact? (Day 4)

**Actions:**
- Cover immediate duties
- Monitor for Day 4 contact
- Prepare for potential fit note if absence extends beyond 7 days

### Scenario 2: Employee Calls with Back Pain

**Questions to emphasize:**
- What action have they taken? (GP visit, painkillers)
- Have they seen a GP or physiotherapist?
- How is it affecting their ability to work?

**Actions:**
- **Consider Wellbeing Support Meeting** within first 2 weeks
- Potential for OH physiotherapy referral
- Discuss potential phased return or lighter duties
- Inform them about PAM Assist

### Scenario 3: Employee Calls Reporting Stress/Anxiety

**Questions to emphasize:**
- Are there any workplace issues contributing?
- Are they aware of EAP services?
- Have they sought medical advice?

**Actions:**
- **Immediate**: Inform about PAM Assist (0800 247 1100)
- **Consider**: Wellbeing Support Meeting within first 2 weeks
- **Assess**: Whether work-related stress risk assessment needed
- **Plan**: For supportive return to work discussion
- **Refer**: Potentially to Able Futures for long-term support

### Scenario 4: Employee Calls About Caring for Sick Child

**Questions to emphasize:**
- Is this a one-off or likely to continue?
- Any other childcare arrangements possible?

**Actions:**
- Consider **Dependents Leave** (not sickness absence)
- Review relevant leave policies
- Discuss flexible working if ongoing issue

## Best Practice Tips

### ‚úÖ DO

- **Be empathetic and supportive** - Employee is unwell and may be anxious
- **Listen actively** - Allow employee to explain their situation
- **Ask open questions** - Get full picture of the situation
- **Document thoroughly** - For return to work discussion and potential reviews
- **Offer support** - Signpost to EAP, OH, or other services
- **Be clear** - About when they should next make contact
- **Consider coverage** - Make arrangements for their duties
- **Follow up** - If you don't hear from them as expected

### ‚ùå DON'T

- **Interrogate** - Keep questions appropriate and supportive
- **Diagnose** - You're not a medical professional
- **Express doubt** - Even if you have concerns about absence pattern
- **Discuss in public** - Take call in private area
- **Share details** - Maintain confidentiality (only share on need-to-know basis)
- **Make them feel guilty** - Even if absence causes coverage issues
- **Forget to document** - You'll need this for return to work discussion
