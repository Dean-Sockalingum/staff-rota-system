## 7Ô∏è‚É£ Attendance Reviews ‚Äì When They Happen

### Review Thresholds

An **Attendance Review** will be arranged if you have:

| **Threshold Type** | **What Triggers Review** |
|-------------------|--------------------------|

| **Working Days Lost** | 6 working days in 6 months OR 8 working days in 12 months |
| **Unauthorised Absence** | Any period of unauthorised absence |

> **Note**: Certified absence criteria is **not** applied pro-rata. Working days criteria **is** applied pro-rata for part-time staff.

### What to Expect

‚úÖ Review arranged **promptly** after the triggering absence  
‚úÖ You may be **accompanied** by a colleague or union representative  
‚úÖ Meeting will be **recorded** using appropriate paperwork  
‚úÖ Decisions will be **current and relevant** to your situation

## 1Ô∏è‚É£1Ô∏è‚É£ Occupational Health (OH)

### What Occupational Health Does

The **Occupational Health Provider** gives independent medical advice to management about:

‚úì Impact of your health condition on your ability to do your job  
‚úì Likelihood of full recovery and timeframe  
‚úì Timescale for return to work  
‚úì Duties you may be capable of performing  
‚úì Whether Equality Act 2010 applies (disability)  
‚úì Reasonable adjustments to support your return/attendance

### Important to Understand

- **OH provides advice** to management ‚Äì they don't diagnose or treat
- **Your GP/NHS** is responsible for diagnosis and treatment
- **Management makes decisions** ‚Äì not Occupational Health
- **You'll receive a copy** of the OH report from your manager
- **Discuss the report** with your manager after receiving it

### Musculoskeletal Referrals

For **untreated musculoskeletal conditions** (back pain, joint issues):

1. OH will **triage** your referral
2. May include **telephone and/or face-to-face consultation**
3. You may receive **exercise advice** to improve your condition
4. Some cases may involve **physical manipulation/physiotherapy**

> **Note**: Most musculoskeletal referrals involve telephone advice from a physiotherapist, not always hands-on treatment.

## 1Ô∏è‚É£4Ô∏è‚É£ Types of Sickness Absence

### Two Categories

| **Category** | **Definition** |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Short-Term Intermittent** | Any absence up to and including **19 working days** |
| **Long-Term Absence** | Any single absence of **20 or more working days** |

> **Note**: Pro-rated for non-standard working patterns

### Equality Act 2010 and Disability

Managers must be mindful of the **Equality Act 2010** when managing absence. You're considered **disabled** if you have:

- A **physical or mental impairment**
- That has a **substantial and long-term adverse effect**
- On your ability to **carry out normal day-to-day activities**

#### What This Means

- **Impairment**: Physical or mental (or both)
- **Long-term**: Lasting at least a year, or likely to last for life, or recur
- **Substantial**: More than minor (may fluctuate or change)
- **Day-to-day activities**: Things you do in your own time and at work

> **Complex Cases**: Managing disability-related absence can be complex. Your manager will consult with Strategic HR for guidance.

## 1Ô∏è‚É£9Ô∏è‚É£ Capability Considerations

### When This Applies

If a **return to work** or **acceptable attendance level** cannot be achieved, capability considerations may begin.

### What Might Lead to This

- Regular spells of **short-term intermittent absence**
- **Long-term absence** (single spell or multiple spells)
- **Combination** of short and long-term absences
- **Specific patterns** of absences

### No Single Threshold

> **Important**: There's no fixed threshold for capability considerations. Every situation is unique based on your health, duration/spells of absence, and capacity to work.

### The Process

**Step 1**: Formal notification that:
- There's concern about your attendance
- Your continued employment is at risk

**Step 2**: Explore all options to support improved attendance:

‚úì Additional training/retraining  
‚úì Occupational Health advice  
‚úì Reasonable time off for rehabilitation/treatment  
‚úì Alternative or restricted duties  
‚úì Flexible/part-time working  
‚úì Phased return to work  
‚úì Redeployment opportunities  
‚úì Redesign of duties

> **This list is not exhaustive** ‚Äì all reasonable options will be considered.

**Step 3**: If options aren't viable and acceptable attendance can't be achieved:
- Management **may consider** termination on grounds of capability
- If you're in the **Local Government Pension Scheme**, eligibility for ill-health retirement assessed first

### Before Any Dismissal Decision

Management must fully address:

‚úÖ Full consideration of why employment cannot be sustained?  
‚úÖ Have you been fully consulted about your health and ability to attend regularly?  
‚úÖ Has **up-to-date medical advice** been obtained and considered?  
‚úÖ Taking into account your **length of service**, has enough support been provided?

> **Critical**: Managers **must consult** with Service Strategic HR before any dismissal decision.

### Your Right to Appeal

You have the **right to appeal** against dismissal on capability grounds under the **Discipline & Appeals Procedure**.

## ‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q: Will I be disciplined for being sick?**  
A: No. Genuine sickness is not a disciplinary matter. This is a **capability policy**, not disciplinary. However, breaches of the reporting process or fraudulent sick leave claims are disciplinary matters.

**Q: Can I be dismissed for sickness absence?**  
A: Only as a last resort after all reasonable support options have been exhausted, and only on **capability grounds** (not misconduct). You would have the right to appeal.

**Q: Will I lose my sick pay?**  
A: No, your sickness allowance entitlements are protected under your Conditions of Service. This policy aims to **protect these provisions** through fair management.

**Q: Can I bring someone to an Attendance Review?**  
A: Yes. You may be accompanied by a **colleague or trade union representative** to any formal meeting.

### Reporting Questions

**Q: Can I text or email instead of calling?**  
A: No. **Phone calls are required** on days 1, 4, 7, and every 7 days thereafter.

**Q: What if my manager isn't available when I call?**  
A: Follow your local arrangements (leave message, call deputy, etc.). Your manager will call you back.

**Q: Do I need a sick note from day 1?**  
A: Not usually. Self-certification applies initially. Your manager will advise if a GP fit note is required (typically for absences over 7 days).

### Support Questions

**Q: Will Occupational Health tell my manager everything?**  
A: OH provides **management advice**, not detailed medical history. They focus on your **fitness for work** and what adjustments might help.

**Q: Is EAP really confidential?**  
A: Yes. PAM Assist won't share information without your **explicit consent**. You can use the service without your employer knowing.

**Q: Can I refuse an OH referral?**  
A: You can, but it's not in your best interests. OH referrals help management understand how to support you. Refusing may limit the options available.

### Return to Work Questions

**Q: Can I return part-time after long-term absence?**  
A: Possibly. **Phased returns** (up to 4 weeks, fully paid) are available. Longer-term part-time work would need to be considered based on operational needs.

**Q: What if I feel I'm being forced back too early?**  
A: Speak to your manager, your GP, and consider an OH referral. You should only return when **fit to do so**.

**Q: Do I have to attend a return to work discussion?**  
A: Yes, it's part of the policy. It's informal, supportive, and ensures you're fit to work.

### Disability Questions

**Q: I have a long-term health condition. Am I protected?**  
A: Possibly. If your condition meets the **Equality Act 2010 definition of disability**, you have additional protections and rights to reasonable adjustments.

**Q: What are reasonable adjustments?**  
A: Changes to your work, workplace, or working arrangements that help you do your job despite your disability. Examples: altered duties, flexible hours, equipment, redeployment.

**Q: How do I know if the Equality Act applies to me?**  
A: Speak to your manager or Strategic HR. An OH referral can help assess this.
