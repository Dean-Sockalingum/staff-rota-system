## üìã Common Scenarios & Solutions

### Scenario 1: Frequent Short Absences

**Employee has 3 absences in 6 months**

**Actions:**
1. Review attendance pattern
2. Conduct return to work discussions after each absence
3. Arrange Attendance Review meeting
4. Consider wellbeing support meeting
5. Explore if EAP or OH referral needed
6. Document everything

**Guides:** [Attendance Review Guide](MANAGERS_ATTENDANCE_REVIEW_GUIDE.md), [Checklist: Attendance Review](CHECKLIST_ATTENDANCE_REVIEW.md)

### Scenario 2: Long-Term Sickness

**Employee absent 20+ working days**

**Actions:**
1. Maintain regular contact (every 4-6 weeks)
2. Refer to Occupational Health
3. Discuss return to work planning
4. Consider phased return
5. Explore reasonable adjustments
6. Keep employee updated on workplace matters

**Guides:** [Manager's Attendance Guide](MANAGERS_ATTENDANCE_GUIDE.md), [OH Referral Guide](MANAGERS_OH_REFERRAL_GUIDE.md), [Reasonable Adjustments Guide](MANAGERS_REASONABLE_ADJUSTMENTS_GUIDE.md)

### Scenario 3: Stress-Related Absence

**Employee reports stress/anxiety/depression**

**Actions:**
1. Conduct wellbeing support meeting within 2 weeks
2. Complete stress risk assessment
3. Remind about EAP services (PAM Assist)
4. Consider OH referral
5. Identify workplace stressors
6. Implement adjustments

**Guides:** [Disability, Mental Health & Stress Guide](MANAGERS_DISABILITY_MH_GUIDE.md), [Reasonable Adjustments Guide](MANAGERS_REASONABLE_ADJUSTMENTS_GUIDE.md)

### Scenario 4: Menopause-Related Absence

**Employee experiencing menopause symptoms affecting work**

**Actions:**
1. Have supportive, confidential conversation
2. Review [Menopause Guide](MANAGERS_MENOPAUSE_GUIDE.md)
3. Consider adjustments: flexible hours, temperature control, breaks
4. Refer to OH if symptoms severe
5. Signpost to EAP and menopause resources

**Guides:** [Menopause in the Workplace Guide](MANAGERS_MENOPAUSE_GUIDE.md)

### Scenario 5: Disability-Related Absence

**Employee has condition covered by Equality Act 2010**

**Actions:**
1. Understand legal obligations
2. Refer to OH for adjustment recommendations
3. Implement reasonable adjustments
4. Monitor effectiveness
5. Regular reviews
6. Don't treat disability absence same as general sickness

**Guides:** [Disability, Mental Health & Stress Guide](MANAGERS_DISABILITY_MH_GUIDE.md), [Reasonable Adjustments Guide](MANAGERS_REASONABLE_ADJUSTMENTS_GUIDE.md)

## üîß Support Services Overview

### Employee Assistance Provider (EAP) - PAM Assist

**What:** Confidential counseling and information service  
**When to use:** Psychological conditions, stress, personal issues  
**How to access:** Employee self-refers or you can recommend  
**Manager action:** Remind employees about service, promote use

### Occupational Health (OH)

**What:** Independent medical advice on fitness for work  
**When to use:** Need advice on adjustments, fitness, prognosis  
**How to access:** Manager refers via OHIO system  
**Manager action:** Make timely referrals, discuss OH reports with employee

### Able Futures / Access to Work

**What:** Government support for workplace adjustments  
**When to use:** Disability or health condition affecting work  
**How to access:** Through Access to Work scheme  
**Manager action:** Signpost employees, support applications

### Strategic HR

**What:** Policy guidance and complex case support  
**When to use:** Capability considerations, dismissals, complex situations  
**How to access:** Contact your service HR team  
**Manager action:** Consult early, before major decisions

### Automatic Review Triggers

| **Threshold** | **Action Required** |
|--------------|---------------------|
| **3 certified absences in 6 months** | Attendance Review Meeting |
| **5 certified absences in 12 months** | Attendance Review Meeting |
| **6 working days in 6 months** (pro-rata) | Attendance Review Meeting |
| **8 working days in 12 months** (pro-rata) | Attendance Review Meeting |
| **Any unauthorised absence** | Attendance Review Meeting |

### Additional Considerations

**Patterns:** Look for patterns like Monday/Friday absences, pre/post leave  
**Disability:** Separate disability-related absence from general sickness  
**Industrial Injury:** Separate allowance - doesn't count against normal sickness  
**Terminal Illness:** Dying to Work Charter - compassionate management

## üí° Best Practice Tips

### Building a Supportive Culture

1. **Normalize wellbeing conversations** - Don't wait for absences
2. **Promote resources proactively** - EAP, OH, support groups
3. **Lead by example** - Model healthy work-life balance
4. **Create psychological safety** - Employees feel safe raising concerns
5. **Regular check-ins** - 1-2-1s that include wellbeing
6. **Flexible where possible** - Work arrangements that support health
7. **Recognize good attendance** - Balance challenge with appreciation

### Effective Communication

- **Be clear and consistent** in your expectations
- **Listen actively** to understand root causes
- **Show empathy** while maintaining professionalism
- **Document thoroughly** but sensitively
- **Follow up** on commitments made
- **Provide regular feedback** on attendance concerns

### Early Intervention

- **Act early** - Don't wait for problems to escalate
- **Wellbeing meetings** within 2 weeks for psych/MSK conditions
- **Stress assessments** at first sign of work-related stress
- **OH referrals** when fitness for work is unclear
- **EAP promotion** before crisis point
- **Adjustments** considered proactively

## ‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

### General Management

**Q: How often should I conduct return to work discussions?**  
A: After EVERY period of absence, regardless of length. Ideally on day of return or as soon as practicable.

**Q: Can I ask for medical evidence from day 1?**  
A: Self-certification applies for first 7 days. Fit note required from day 8. However, can request earlier if pattern of absence concerns.

**Q: What if an employee refuses an OH referral?**  
A: They can refuse, but explain it limits your ability to support them and make informed decisions about adjustments or capability.

### Equality Act & Disability

**Q: How do I know if Equality Act applies?**  
A: Substantial, long-term (12+ months) adverse effect on day-to-day activities. OH can advise. Seek Strategic HR guidance.

**Q: Do I have to make every adjustment an employee requests?**  
A: Only "reasonable" adjustments. Consider effectiveness, cost, practicality, impact on service. OH and HR can advise.

**Q: Can I dismiss someone with a disability for poor attendance?**  
A: Very complex - must exhaust all reasonable adjustments first. Always consult Strategic HR before any capability action involving disability.

### Procedures

**Q: What if I miss an attendance review trigger?**  
A: Arrange it as soon as identified. Don't delay further. Explain the delay to employee.

**Q: Can an employee bring someone to a return to work discussion?**  
A: It's informal so no automatic right, but good practice to allow if they request (unless abusing process).

**Q: How long can a phased return last?**  
A: Up to 4 weeks, fully paid. Can be recommended by OH, GP, or agreed between you and employee.

### Complex Cases

**Q: Employee has terminal diagnosis - what do I do?**  
A: Contact Strategic HR immediately. Dying to Work Charter applies - manage with dignity and compassion. Won't dismiss due to diagnosis unless in their best interests.

**Q: Pattern of Monday/Friday absences - can I challenge this?**  
A: Yes, if genuine concern. Discuss pattern at return to work/review. May indicate workplace issues or abuse. OH referral may help clarify.

**Q: Employee refuses to engage with support - what now?**  
A: Document lack of engagement. This becomes part of capability consideration. But continue offering support and explaining consequences.
