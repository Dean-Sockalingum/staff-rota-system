## Purpose

This SOP defines procedures for requesting, approving, and tracking annual leave to ensure fair allocation, adequate staffing levels, and compliance with employment law.

## Scope

- Annual leave requests and approvals
- Leave balance tracking and calculations
- Carry-over and pro-rata entitlements
- Leave usage targets and monitoring
- Clash detection and management

## Annual Leave Entitlements

### Standard Entitlements

**Full-Time Staff (37.5 hours/week):**
- **Years 0-5:** 28 days (including 8 public holidays)
- **Years 5-10:** 31 days (including 8 public holidays)
- **Years 10+:** 34 days (including 8 public holidays)

**Part-Time Staff:**
- Pro-rata based on contracted hours
- Example: 20 hours/week = 53% of full-time = 14.84 days (Years 0-5)

**Public Holidays:**
- 8 days included in entitlement (New Year, Easter, May Day, Summer, Christmas, Boxing Day, etc.)
- If working public holiday: Time off in lieu (TOIL) added

---

### Pro-Rata Calculations

**Mid-Year Starters:**

System auto-calculates based on:
```
Start Date: 15 June 2025
Days Remaining in Year: 200 days (approx 6.5 months)
Full Entitlement: 28 days
Pro-Rata: (200/365) Ã— 28 = 15.34 days (rounds to 15 days)
```

**Leavers:**

Entitlement calculated to leaving date:
```
Leaving Date: 31 August 2025
Days Worked: 242 days (approx 8 months)
Used: 12 days
Entitlement: (242/365) Ã— 28 = 18.56 days
Owed: 18.56 - 12 = 6.56 days (paid out with final salary)
```

---

### Carry-Over Rules

**Standard Policy:**
- Maximum 5 days carry-over to next year
- Must be used by 31 March of new year
- Requires manager approval
- Exceptional circumstances: Up to 10 days with HR approval

**Why Limit Carry-Over?**
- Prevents staff exhaustion (not taking breaks)
- Ensures regular time off for wellbeing
- Avoids large liability on accounts
- Employment law best practice

**Benefits:**
- Ensures staff take regular breaks (wellbeing)
- Prevents burnout
- Identifies staff not taking leave (may indicate problems)
- Avoids year-end rush (everyone wanting December off)
- Spreads leave throughout year (easier to cover)

## Special Leave Scenarios

### Compassionate Leave

**Not annual leave - separate entitlement:**
- Bereavement: 3-5 days paid
- Serious family illness: Up to 5 days paid
- Emergency domestic: 1-2 days paid

**How to Request:**
- Mark as "Compassionate Leave" not "Annual Leave"
- Doesn't deduct from annual leave balance
- Requires evidence (funeral details, hospital admission, etc.)
- Manager approval required but usually granted

---

### Parental Leave

**Maternity/Paternity Leave:**
- Separate statutory entitlement
- Not annual leave
- Managed through HR, not this system

**Annual Leave During Mat/Pat Leave:**
- Continue to accrue annual leave while on mat/pat leave
- Can be taken before or after mat/pat leave
- Or paid out if not taken

---

### Sick Leave

**If Sick While on Annual Leave:**

1. **Notify Manager Immediately**
   - Call/text as soon as unwell
   - Explain illness

2. **Provide Sick Note** (if >7 days)
   - GP sick note
   - Emailed to manager and HR

3. **Leave Days Returned**
   - System converts leave days to sick leave
   - Annual leave days returned to balance
   - Can rebook those days later

**Example:**
```
Booked: 10-14 June (5 days annual leave)
Sick: 12-14 June (3 days)
Result: 10-11 June = Annual leave (2 days used)
        12-14 June = Sick leave (3 days returned to balance)
Remaining Balance: +3 days
```

---

### Time Off In Lieu (TOIL)

**Earned When:**
- Working on public holiday
- Extra hours for on-call/emergency
- Approved by manager

**How to Use:**
1. TOIL hours added to balance (separate from annual leave)
2. Request leave as "TOIL" not "Annual Leave"
3. Usually must be used within 3 months
4. Cannot be carried over to next year
5. Not paid out if unused (use it or lose it)

## Year-End Procedures

### October: Year-End Planning

**For Managers:**

1. **Run Leave Usage Report**
   - Show all staff with remaining balance
   - Identify those <50% used

2. **Individual Meetings**
   - Staff with <50% used: "You need to book [X] days by 31 December"
   - Discuss preferred dates
   - Check for any issues preventing leave

3. **Set Expectations**
   - "Everyone must use at least 80% of leave by year end"
   - "Maximum 5 days carry-over"
   - "Book December leave by end of October"

---

### November: Christmas Planning

**Rotation System for Christmas/New Year:**

Year 1: J. Smith, K. Brown get Christmas Eve - Boxing Day (3 days)
        M. Miller, L. Wilson get 28 Dec - 2 Jan (5 days)

Year 2: Swap
        M. Miller, L. Wilson get Christmas period
        J. Smith, K. Brown get New Year period

**Compulsory Closure Days:**
- 25 December (Christmas Day)
- 26 December (Boxing Day)
- 1 January (New Year's Day)

All staff must book these as annual leave (deducted from entitlement).

---

### December: Final Push

**Deadline: 20 December**
- All leave for current year must be requested by 20 Dec
- After 20 Dec: Too late, unused leave may be forfeited
- Exception: Carry-over (up to 5 days) with manager approval

**System Lockdown:**
- After 31 December, cannot request backdated leave
- Unused leave >5 days = forfeited
- Managers must ensure team compliance

---

### January: New Year Start

**Automatic Processes:**

1. **Leave Balances Reset** (1 January)
   - New year entitlement added
   - Previous year balance archived
   - Carry-over days added (max 5)
   - Pro-rata calculated for starters

2. **Carry-Over Tracking**
   - Separate balance: "Carry-Over: 3 days (use by 31 March)"
   - System alerts if not used by deadline
   - Automatically forfeited if unused by 31 March

## Compliance & Legal

### Employment Law Requirements

**Minimum Leave:**
- 5.6 weeks (28 days for full-time) by law
- Must be taken (cannot be bought out except on leaving)
- Employees can be compelled to take leave
- Notice period: 2Ã— length of leave (e.g., 2 weeks notice for 1 week leave)

**Records:**
- Must keep leave records for 2 years
- Inspections by HMRC/tribunal cases
- System provides audit trail

---

### Discrimination & Fairness

**Equal Treatment:**
- Leave allocation must be fair and transparent
- No discrimination based on protected characteristics
- Part-time staff: Pro-rata entitlement (not less favorable)
- Consistent approval criteria

**Religious/Cultural Leave:**
- Should accommodate where possible
- Balance operational needs with religious observance
- Document reasons if declining

## Troubleshooting

### Issue: "Insufficient balance" error

**Cause:** Trying to book more days than available

**Solution:**
1. Check your balance (Dashboard or Annual Leave page)
2. Reduce number of days requested
3. Or wait until new year when balance resets

---

### Issue: Request showing as Pending for >1 week

**Cause:** Manager hasn't reviewed yet

**Solution:**
1. Speak to manager directly
2. Send polite reminder: "Could you review my leave request when you have a moment?"
3. If urgent and manager unavailable: Contact senior manager
4. Check request hasn't gone to spam (if manager uses email notifications)

---

### Issue: Need to cancel approved leave

**Situation:** Leave approved but plans changed

**Solution:**
1. Navigate to Annual Leave â†’ My Requests
2. Find approved request
3. Click **"Cancel Request"**
4. Add reason: "Plans changed - will rebook later"
5. Days returned to balance immediately
6. Notify manager (courtesy)

**Deadline:** Can cancel up to day before leave starts

---

### Issue: Wrong dates booked

**Solution:**
1. Cancel incorrect request
2. Submit new request with correct dates
3. Or contact manager to amend manually
4. Check system calendar to avoid mistakes

---

### Issue: Public holiday not recognized

**Cause:** Working on public holiday but system didn't give TOIL

**Solution:**
1. Contact manager/HR
2. Manual TOIL adjustment required
3. System admin adds TOIL hours to your balance
4. You can then use as leave later
