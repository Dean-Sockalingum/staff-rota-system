# Manager's Attendance Review Meeting Checklist

## Introduction

This checklist sets out what you'll need to do both **before** and **during** a formal attendance review meeting.

**When to use this checklist:**
- The employee's absence record has **breached Council trigger points**
- You need to conduct a formal meeting to discuss attendance concerns
- You're planning management action due to unsustainable attendance levels

**This is a formal process.** The employee has the **right to be accompanied** by a colleague or trade union representative.

> **üí° Important:** An attendance review meeting is different from a return to work discussion. This is a formal meeting triggered by attendance thresholds being met or exceeded.

See also: [Supporting Attendance: Managers Guide](./MANAGERS_ATTENDANCE_GUIDE.md) for full context.

## Before the Meeting

### 1. Confirm the Arrangements

| Task | Details | ‚úì |
|------|---------|---|
| **Send formal invitation** | At least 5 working days' notice<br>Include: date, time, location, reason, trigger point breached | ‚ñ° |
| **Advise of right to be accompanied** | By colleague or trade union representative<br>**Employee's responsibility** to arrange this | ‚ñ° |
| **Arrange private setting** | Quiet room, no interruptions<br>Consider remote option if necessary | ‚ñ° |
| **Allow enough time** | Minimum 1 hour (may need longer)<br>Don't schedule back-to-back meetings | ‚ñ° |

### 2. Prepare Your Documentation

Gather and review:

- ‚úÖ **Complete attendance record** (last 12 months minimum)
- ‚úÖ **All absence certificates** (self-certs and fit notes)
- ‚úÖ **Return to work discussion notes**
- ‚úÖ **Previous attendance review notes** (if any)
- ‚úÖ **OH reports** (if any referrals made)
- ‚úÖ **Copy of Supporting Attendance Policy**
- ‚úÖ **Trigger points reference** (which one was breached)
- ‚úÖ **Blank formal interview checklist form**

### 3. Consider the Context

Think about:

| Question | Why It Matters |
|----------|----------------|
| **Are absences related to a disability?** | Equality Act 2010 protection applies<br>Different approach needed |
| **Is there an OH report?** | What were the recommendations?<br>Have they been implemented? |
| **Has the employee engaged?** | Attended RTW discussions?<br>Followed reporting procedures? |
| **Are there workplace issues?** | Could work be contributing factor?<br>Stress, bullying, workload? |
| **What support has been offered?** | EAP, Able Futures, adjustments?<br>Has the employee engaged with support? |

### 4. Plan Your Approach

**What outcome are you considering?**

- ‚ö†Ô∏è Formal written warning about attendance
- üîÑ Continued monitoring with review date
- üè• OH referral (if not already done)
- ü§ù Reasonable adjustments to support improvement
- üìã Capability procedure (if attendance unsustainable)
- ‚ùå Disciplinary action (if misconduct - unauthorized absence)

## At the Meeting

### Opening the Meeting (First 5-10 Minutes)

| Step | What to Say/Do | ‚úì |
|------|----------------|---|
| **Welcome** | Thank them for attending<br>Introduce attendees (note-taker, if present) | ‚ñ° |
| **Set expectations** | This is a formal meeting<br>Explain structure and timing | ‚ñ° |
| **Explain purpose** | "Your attendance record has breached the trigger point of [X]"<br>State which threshold clearly | ‚ñ° |
| **Confirm right to be accompanied** | Check if they have representation<br>Allow time if they wish to wait | ‚ñ° |

### Discussion Points (Main Part of Meeting)

| Topic | What to Cover | Questions to Ask | ‚úì |
|-------|---------------|------------------|---|
| **1. Confirm trigger breach** | State specific threshold<br>e.g., "4 separate absences in 6 months" | - | ‚ñ° |
| **2. Explore reasons** | Review each absence period<br>Look for patterns | "Can you explain the reasons for each absence?"<br>"Is there an underlying condition?" | ‚ñ° |
| **3. Overall attendance level** | Discuss total days lost<br>Impact over time | "Are you aware how many days you've been absent?"<br>"Do you understand the concern?" | ‚ñ° |
| **4. Impact on service** | Service delivery disruption<br>Effect on colleagues<br>Increased workload on others | "Do you understand the impact of your absence on the team?"<br>"How do you think this affects service users?" | ‚ñ° |
| **5. Listen to responses** | **Active listening**<br>Allow employee to explain<br>Consider circumstances | "Is there anything else you'd like to tell me?"<br>"What support do you need?" | ‚ñ° |
| **6. Areas of support** | What has helped?<br>What could help? | "Have you used the EAP?"<br>"Would OH referral be helpful?"<br>"Do you need adjustments?" | ‚ñ° |
| **7. Areas of concern** | Patterns (e.g., Mondays/Fridays)<br>Unauthorized absences<br>Late reporting | "I've noticed absences tend to fall on [pattern] - can you explain?"<br>"Why was absence not reported on Day 1?" | ‚ñ° |
| **8. OH/EAP referral** | If not done, consider now<br>If done, review recommendations | "I'd like to refer you to OH to explore support"<br>"Have you followed OH advice?" | ‚ñ° |
| **9. Disciplinary action** | **Only for misconduct:**<br>Unauthorized absence<br>Failure to follow procedure | "You failed to report absence - this is a breach of contract"<br>Separate disciplinary process | ‚ñ° |
| **10. Advise of consequences** | **Be clear:**<br>Further absences may lead to action<br>Attendance must improve | "I need to make it clear that if your attendance doesn't improve, further management action will be taken"<br>"The Council expects sustained improvement" | ‚ñ° |

### Closing the Meeting (Last 10-15 Minutes)

| Step | What to Say/Do | ‚úì |
|------|----------------|---|
| **Summarize discussion** | Key points raised<br>Employee's explanations<br>Support discussed | ‚ñ° |
| **State your decision** | Outcome (see below)<br>Next steps<br>Review period | ‚ñ° |
| **Explain monitoring** | How long you'll monitor<br>What you expect to see<br>When you'll review | ‚ñ° |
| **Confirm in writing** | Notes will be sent within 5 days<br>Right to comment on accuracy | ‚ñ° |
| **Answer questions** | Allow employee to clarify<br>Check understanding | ‚ñ° |
| **Set review date** | Book next meeting now<br>Usually 3-6 months | ‚ñ° |

## Recording the Outcome

### What to Record

Complete the **formal/follow-up interview checklist** with:

1. ‚úÖ **Date, time, attendees** (including representatives)
2. ‚úÖ **Trigger point breached** (specific threshold)
3. ‚úÖ **Attendance record summary** (total days/instances)
4. ‚úÖ **Reasons for absence** (as explained by employee)
5. ‚úÖ **Impact discussed** (service, colleagues, workload)
6. ‚úÖ **Employee's responses** (key points, context, explanations)
7. ‚úÖ **Support offered/discussed** (EAP, OH, adjustments)
8. ‚úÖ **Outcome/decision** (warning, monitoring, capability, etc.)
9. ‚úÖ **Further action planned** (OH referral, adjustments, review)
10. ‚úÖ **Review date** (when you'll assess progress)
11. ‚úÖ **Expectations** (what improvement looks like)

### Follow Up in Writing

**Within 5 working days**, send written confirmation including:

- Summary of meeting discussion
- Decision/outcome
- Expectations for improvement
- Monitoring period and review date
- Support available (EAP, OH, adjustments)
- Consequences of no improvement
- Right to comment on accuracy (usually 10 days)
- Right to appeal (if applicable)

## Possible Outcomes

### Outcome 1: Continued Monitoring with Support

**When:**
- Employee has genuine health issues
- Employee is engaging with support
- OH recommendations in place or pending
- Reasonable to expect improvement

**Action:**
- ‚úÖ Continue to monitor attendance
- ‚úÖ Set review date (usually 3-6 months)
- ‚úÖ Offer/continue support (EAP, OH, adjustments)
- ‚úÖ Explain expectations clearly
- ‚úÖ Confirm consequences if no improvement

**Example:** *"I'm going to continue monitoring your attendance for the next 6 months. I expect to see sustained improvement, with no further trigger point breaches. We'll review again in [date]. If there's no improvement, I'll need to consider capability procedures."*

---

### Outcome 2: Formal Warning

**When:**
- Attendance is cause for concern
- Employee has not engaged with support
- No acceptable reason for poor attendance
- Previous informal discussions have not worked

**Action:**
- ‚úÖ Issue formal written warning
- ‚úÖ Set clear expectations for improvement
- ‚úÖ Set monitoring period (usually 12 months)
- ‚úÖ Confirm consequences (further action if no improvement)
- ‚úÖ Set review date
- ‚úÖ Offer support (even with warning)

**Example:** *"I'm issuing you with a formal written warning. Your attendance must improve immediately. I expect to see no trigger point breaches in the next 12 months. If your attendance doesn't improve, the next step will be consideration of your capability to fulfill your role."*

---

### Outcome 3: Refer to Occupational Health

**When:**
- Health concerns not yet explored
- Need expert medical advice
- Employee has underlying condition
- Want to explore adjustments/support

**Action:**
- ‚úÖ Explain why OH referral is needed
- ‚úÖ Obtain consent (usually required)
- ‚úÖ Complete OH referral form
- ‚úÖ Ask specific questions to OH
- ‚úÖ Adjourn meeting to await OH report (if needed)
- ‚úÖ Reconvene after OH report received

**Example:** *"I'd like to refer you to Occupational Health to get expert advice on how we can support you. This will help us understand if there are any adjustments we can make. I'm going to adjourn this meeting until we receive their report, then we'll reconvene to discuss next steps."*

---

### Outcome 4: Consider Reasonable Adjustments

**When:**
- Disability or long-term health condition
- OH report recommends adjustments
- Adjustments could support improvement
- Equality Act 2010 duty to consider

**Action:**
- ‚úÖ Discuss specific adjustments with employee
- ‚úÖ Agree adjustments in writing
- ‚úÖ Set trial period (usually 3-6 months)
- ‚úÖ Review effectiveness regularly
- ‚úÖ Monitor attendance during trial

**Examples of adjustments:**
- üïí Flexible start/finish times
- üè† Working from home options
- ü™ë Ergonomic equipment
- ‚è∞ Reduced hours (if appropriate)
- üìÖ Adjusted duties (temporarily)

**Example:** *"Based on the OH report, I'm going to agree to flexible start times between 8-10am for a trial period of 3 months. We'll review whether this is helping. I still need to see overall improvement in your attendance during this time."*

---

### Outcome 5: Move to Capability Procedure

**When:**
- Attendance is unsustainable
- Employee unable to meet attendance requirements
- All support has been offered and tried
- No reasonable expectation of improvement

**Action:**
- ‚úÖ Explain unsustainability
- ‚úÖ Confirm all support offered
- ‚úÖ Set out capability process
- ‚úÖ Consider redeployment options
- ‚úÖ Consider ill-health retirement (if applicable)
- ‚úÖ Follow formal capability procedure

**Example:** *"I've concluded that your attendance level is unsustainable and you're unable to meet the requirements of your role. We've offered all available support and there's been no sustained improvement. I'm now moving to the capability procedure. This could ultimately result in dismissal on grounds of capability."*

**‚ö†Ô∏è Important:** Capability process is serious. Seek HR advice before proceeding.

---

### Outcome 6: Disciplinary Action (Misconduct)

**When:**
- **Unauthorized absence** (failure to report)
- **Breach of reporting procedures** (persistent)
- **Dishonesty** (false reasons for absence)

**Action:**
- ‚úÖ This is a **separate** disciplinary matter
- ‚úÖ Follow Council's disciplinary procedure
- ‚úÖ Arrange disciplinary hearing
- ‚úÖ Potential outcomes: warning, final warning, dismissal

**Example:** *"You failed to report your absence on Day 1 on [dates]. This is a breach of contract and reporting procedures. I'm arranging a separate disciplinary hearing to address this misconduct."*

**‚ö†Ô∏è Note:** Disciplinary is for **failure to follow procedures**, not for being genuinely ill.

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Short-Term Frequent Absences (Trigger Met)

**Situation:**
Employee has had 5 separate short absences in 6 months (trigger is 4). All for different minor illnesses (cold, migraine, stomach upset, etc.). No pattern. All properly reported.

**Questions to Ask:**
- "Can you explain the reason for each of these absences?"
- "Is there an underlying health condition we should be aware of?"
- "Have you seen your GP about these recurring issues?"
- "Are you aware of the EAP service?"
- "Is there anything at work that might be affecting your health?"

**Likely Actions:**
- ‚úÖ Continue monitoring for 6 months
- ‚úÖ Offer EAP referral
- ‚úÖ Consider OH referral if pattern continues
- ‚úÖ Set review date
- ‚úÖ Explain expectations (no further breaches)
- ‚úÖ Confirm consequences (formal warning if continues)

**Example Outcome:** *"I'm going to continue monitoring your attendance for 6 months. I'd like you to contact the EAP to discuss support for your general wellbeing. I expect to see no further trigger point breaches. We'll review again in 6 months. If there's no improvement, I'll need to consider formal action."*

---

### Scenario 2: Long-Term Absence Followed by Return

**Situation:**
Employee had 8-week absence for surgery, returned to work, then has since had 3 separate short absences in 4 months (back pain related to surgery recovery). Total days lost breaches trigger.

**Questions to Ask:**
- "How are you recovering from your surgery?"
- "Are these recent absences related to your surgery?"
- "Did you have a phased return? Is it working?"
- "Have you discussed ongoing pain with your GP?"
- "Would an OH referral be helpful to explore adjustments?"
- "Do you need any adjustments to your duties or workstation?"

**Likely Actions:**
- ‚úÖ **OH referral** (explore ongoing medical issues and adjustments)
- ‚úÖ Adjourn meeting to await OH report
- ‚úÖ Consider adjustments based on OH recommendations
- ‚úÖ Monitor attendance during recovery period
- ‚úÖ Set review date after adjustments in place

**Example Outcome:** *"I'm going to refer you to Occupational Health to get expert advice on your recovery and what adjustments might help. I'm adjourning this meeting until we receive their report. Once we have that, we'll reconvene to discuss what support we can offer."*

---

### Scenario 3: Stress/Mental Health Related Absence

**Situation:**
Employee has had multiple absences totaling 15 days over 6 months, all stress/anxiety related. OH report suggests work-related stress. Employee has engaged with EAP.

**Questions to Ask:**
- "Can you tell me more about what's causing your stress?"
- "Is there anything about your work or workplace that's contributing?"
- "Have you found the EAP helpful?"
- "What would help you manage stress better at work?"
- "Have we completed a stress risk assessment?"
- "Would Able Futures (CBT) be helpful?"

**Likely Actions:**
- ‚úÖ **Stress risk assessment** (if not done)
- ‚úÖ Explore workplace issues (workload, relationships, role)
- ‚úÖ **Able Futures referral** (free CBT for work-related stress)
- ‚úÖ Consider adjustments (flexible working, reduced caseload, etc.)
- ‚úÖ Regular wellbeing check-ins
- ‚úÖ Monitor with reasonable expectation (given mental health)
- ‚úÖ Set review date (3-6 months)

**Example Outcome:** *"I'm going to arrange a stress risk assessment to identify what's causing stress at work. I'm also referring you to Able Futures for cognitive behavioral therapy support. Let's discuss some immediate adjustments to your workload. I'll monitor your attendance over the next 6 months with understanding that you're receiving treatment."*

**‚ö†Ô∏è Important:** Mental health is a protected characteristic. Be sensitive and supportive.

---

### Scenario 4: Pattern of Absences (Mondays/Fridays)

**Situation:**
Employee has 6 absences over 6 months (breaches trigger). 5 out of 6 are on Mondays or Fridays. No medical certificates. All self-certified for minor ailments.

**Questions to Ask:**
- "I've noticed that most of your absences fall on Mondays and Fridays. Can you explain this pattern?"
- "Is there a reason why you tend to be unwell at the start/end of the week?"
- "Is there something about your work schedule that's difficult?"
- "Are there any issues at work that we need to discuss?"
- "Are you finding it difficult to balance work and personal commitments?"

**Likely Actions:**
- ‚úÖ **Address pattern directly** (this may suggest misconduct)
- ‚úÖ Explore if genuine reasons or poor attendance culture
- ‚úÖ **Formal warning** if no acceptable explanation
- ‚úÖ Close monitoring (may require medical certificates for all future absences)
- ‚úÖ Consider disciplinary if pattern continues and no medical evidence
- ‚úÖ Set very clear expectations

**Example Outcome:** *"I'm concerned about the pattern of your absences falling on Mondays and Fridays. I need to see immediate improvement in your attendance. I'm issuing a formal warning. Going forward, I may require medical certificates for all absences, not just those over 7 days. If this pattern continues, I'll consider disciplinary action."*

**‚ö†Ô∏è Be careful:** Don't assume dishonesty, but address concerning patterns clearly.

---

### Scenario 5: Disability-Related Absence

**Situation:**
Employee has diabetes (disclosed disability). Has had 8 absences in 12 months related to diabetes management and complications (hospital appointments, hypos, etc.). Total 20 days lost.

**Questions to Ask:**
- "How are you managing your diabetes?"
- "Are your hospital appointments routine or urgent?"
- "Can any appointments be scheduled outside working hours?"
- "What adjustments would help you manage your condition at work?"
- "Has OH advised on adjustments?"
- "Are there any triggers at work that affect your diabetes?"

**Likely Actions:**
- ‚úÖ **Equality Act 2010 applies** - disability-related absence
- ‚úÖ **Do not include disability-related absence** in trigger point calculation (if clearly related)
- ‚úÖ OH referral to confirm reasonable adjustments
- ‚úÖ Implement adjustments (time off for appointments, breaks to manage blood sugar, etc.)
- ‚úÖ **Disability leave** (if council policy allows for appointments)
- ‚úÖ Monitor non-disability absence separately
- ‚úÖ Regular wellbeing check-ins

**Example Outcome:** *"I understand your absences are related to your diabetes. Under the Equality Act, we need to make reasonable adjustments to support you. I'm going to refer you to OH to confirm what adjustments would help. I'm not including your diabetes-related absences in the trigger point calculation, but I do need to monitor your overall health and capability to do your role."*

**‚ö†Ô∏è Critical:** Seek HR advice. Disability-related absence requires different approach.

---

### Scenario 6: No Engagement from Employee

**Situation:**
Employee has repeatedly breached triggers. Has not attended return to work discussions, refused OH referral, not engaged with EAP. No improvement despite previous attendance review.

**Questions to Ask:**
- "Why have you not attended return to work discussions?"
- "Why did you refuse the OH referral I offered?"
- "Do you understand the seriousness of your attendance record?"
- "Is there a reason you're not engaging with the support we've offered?"
- "What do you think needs to happen now?"

**Likely Actions:**
- ‚úÖ **Formal written warning** (or final warning if repeat)
- ‚úÖ Make it clear: non-engagement is unacceptable
- ‚úÖ **Mandatory OH referral** (explain it's part of their contract)
- ‚úÖ Very close monitoring (short review period - 3 months)
- ‚úÖ **Capability procedure** if no engagement or improvement
- ‚úÖ Consider disciplinary if failure to engage is willful

**Example Outcome:** *"You've repeatedly failed to engage with the support offered and have not attended return to work discussions. This is unacceptable. I'm issuing you with a formal written warning. I'm also referring you to Occupational Health - this is not optional. If you fail to attend the OH appointment or if there's no immediate improvement in your attendance, I'll be moving to capability procedures which could result in dismissal."*

**‚ö†Ô∏è Important:** Non-engagement is a serious issue. Document thoroughly.

## Best Practice Tips

### ‚úÖ DO:

| DO | Why It Matters |
|----|----------------|
| **Be prepared** | Review all documentation thoroughly before meeting<br>Know the attendance record inside out |
| **Be consistent** | Apply policy fairly across all employees<br>Don't make exceptions without good reason |
| **Be clear about triggers** | State exactly which trigger point was breached<br>Use specific numbers and dates |
| **Listen actively** | Give the employee opportunity to explain<br>Consider context and circumstances |
| **Focus on facts** | Discuss actual attendance record, not perceptions<br>Use objective data |
| **Offer support** | Even when issuing warnings, offer EAP/OH support<br>Show you want them to succeed |
| **Explain impact** | Help employee understand how absence affects team/service<br>Make it real, not abstract |
| **Set clear expectations** | Be specific about what improvement looks like<br>Leave no room for misunderstanding |
| **Confirm in writing** | Always follow up within 5 days<br>Document everything |
| **Involve HR** | Seek advice on complex cases (disability, capability)<br>Don't go it alone |

---

### ‚ùå DON'T:

| DON'T | Why It's a Problem |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Don't make assumptions** | Absence might be genuine - don't assume malingering<br>Let evidence guide you |
| **Don't rush** | Allow time for full discussion<br>Don't cut employee off |
| **Don't be dismissive** | Take employee's explanations seriously<br>Show empathy even if taking action |
| **Don't ignore patterns** | If you notice patterns (Mondays, etc.), address them<br>Don't let concerns go unspoken |
| **Don't treat all absence the same** | Disability-related absence needs different approach<br>Understand Equality Act duties |
| **Don't skip OH referral** | If in doubt, refer to OH for expert advice<br>Don't try to diagnose medical issues yourself |
| **Don't make threats** | Be professional - explain consequences, don't threaten<br>"If X happens, then Y" not "I'll get you dismissed" |
| **Don't go off-policy** | Follow the Supporting Attendance Policy<br>Don't invent your own rules |
| **Don't avoid difficult conversations** | Address poor attendance directly<br>Avoiding issues makes them worse |
| **Don't forget employee rights** | Right to be accompanied<br>Right to comment on notes<br>Right to appeal |

## Red Flags - Seek Immediate HR Advice

**üö® Seek HR advice before proceeding if:**

| Red Flag | Why It's Complex |
|----------|------------------|
| **Employee mentions discrimination** | Claims absence related to protected characteristic<br>Claims being treated unfairly compared to others | Potential discrimination claim<br>Equality Act 2010 |
| **Employee has undisclosed disability** | Reveals disability during meeting<br>Could explain absence pattern | Changes everything<br>Reasonable adjustments duty |
| **Grievance raised** | Employee raises grievance about treatment or workplace issue<br>May pause attendance procedure | Grievance may need to be heard first<br>Could be related to absence |
| **Employee becomes distressed** | Breaks down, discloses mental health crisis, mentions self-harm | Safeguarding concern<br>May need to adjourn |
| **Trade union raises concerns** | TU rep challenges process or fairness<br>Threatens action | May indicate procedural issue<br>Need HR support |
| **Pregnancy disclosed** | Employee reveals pregnancy<br>Absences may be pregnancy-related | Pregnancy discrimination law<br>Different trigger approach |
| **Employee requests ill-health retirement** | Believes unable to continue working<br>Wants to retire on health grounds | Specific process and criteria<br>HR and Pensions involvement needed |
| **Capability already seems appropriate** | Attendance clearly unsustainable<br>All support exhausted | Moving to capability is serious<br>HR must be involved |

**Don't proceed alone in these situations - pause and get expert advice.**
