## Introduction

### Purpose of This Checklist

This form sets out what you'll need to do **before and during** the return to work discussion.

### When to Use This

You should carry out a return to work discussion **on the day the employee returns to work** following **any period of absence**.

You can find more information on this in **Supporting Attendance: Manager's Guide**.

## Documentation Requirements

### Forms to Complete

#### 1. Sickness Absence Certificate (Self-Certification)

**For absences of 7 days or less:**

- Employee completes their section (reason for absence, dates)
- You complete manager's section (discussion notes, any actions)
- Both parties sign
- Scan and send to CBS for employee's personal file

#### 2. Statement of Fitness for Work (Fit Note)

**For absences of 8 days or more:**

- Employee should have already submitted this
- Review any recommendations on the fit note:
  - Phased return to work
  - Altered hours
  - Amended duties
  - Workplace adaptations
- Discuss how these will be implemented

## Trigger Points: When to Take Further Action

### Attendance Review Thresholds

If the employee's absence record meets these thresholds, you should arrange a **formal Attendance Review meeting**:

| Threshold Type | Criteria | Action Required |
|---------------|----------|-----------------|
| **Certified Absences** | 3 within 6 months **OR** 5 within 12 months | Formal Attendance Review |
| **Working Days Lost** | 6 working days (or hours equivalent) within 6 months **OR** 8 working days (or hours equivalent) within 12 months | Formal Attendance Review |
| **Long-term Absence** | Over 19 consecutive days | Long Term Absence Meeting |
| **Unauthorised Absence** | Any period | Disciplinary action (not attendance process) |

### When to Consider OH Referral

Consider referring to Occupational Health if:

- ü©∫ Absence has lasted 8-12 weeks with no expected return date
- üîÑ Pattern of frequent short-term absences related to same condition
- ‚ôø Disability or long-term health condition affecting work
- üß† Mental health or stress-related absence
- üí™ Musculoskeletal condition (back pain, RSI, etc.)
- ‚ùì Uncertainty about fitness to return to full duties
- üîß Need professional advice on reasonable adjustments

## Common Scenarios and How to Handle Them

### Scenario 1: First Absence - Simple Illness (Cold/Flu)

**Key Questions:**
- Welcome back - how are you feeling now?
- What was the reason for your absence?
- Are you fully recovered and fit for all duties?
- Anything we can do to support you?

**Actions:**
- Complete self-certification form
- Update them on any work developments
- Thank them for letting you know promptly
- No further action needed

### Scenario 2: Return After Musculoskeletal Problem (Back Pain)

**Key Questions:**
- How is your back now?
- Did you seek medical advice?
- Are there any adjustments that would help? (e.g., ergonomic assessment, different chair, lighter duties temporarily)
- Any workplace factors contributing? (lifting, posture, workstation setup)

**Actions:**
- ‚úÖ Complete self-certification form
- ‚úÖ Consider **Wellbeing Support Meeting** if absence continues
- ‚úÖ Arrange workstation assessment if needed
- ‚úÖ Discuss temporary lighter duties if recommended by GP
- ‚úÖ Consider OH referral if condition likely to recur or affect work long-term

### Scenario 3: Return After Stress/Anxiety

**Key Questions:**
- How are you feeling now?
- Any work-related factors we should be aware of?
- Are you aware of our Employee Assistance Programme? (PAM Assist: 0800 247 1100)
- Would you find it helpful to discuss any workplace adjustments?

**Actions:**
- ‚úÖ Complete self-certification form
- ‚úÖ **Signpost to PAM Assist** for counseling support
- ‚úÖ Consider **Able Futures** (0800 321 3137) for long-term mental health support
- ‚úÖ Discuss any work-related stressors and how to address them
- ‚úÖ Consider **stress risk assessment** if work-related
- ‚úÖ Arrange review meeting in 2-4 weeks to check progress
- ‚úÖ Consider OH referral if ongoing

### Scenario 4: Return After Long-term Absence (Surgery)

**Key Questions:**
- Welcome back - how are you feeling after your recovery?
- Are there any restrictions from your doctor on what you can do?
- Would a phased return be helpful?
- Any adjustments needed to your duties or workspace?

**Actions:**
- ‚úÖ Complete self-certification form (for first 7 days) + review fit note
- ‚úÖ Implement any adjustments recommended in fit note
- ‚úÖ Discuss **phased return** (e.g., reduced hours initially, building up over 2-4 weeks)
- ‚úÖ Confirm adjustments in writing
- ‚úÖ Schedule **regular review meetings** (e.g., weekly for first month)
- ‚úÖ Keep in touch with OH if already involved

### Scenario 5: Approaching Attendance Review Threshold

**Key Questions:**
- (All standard return to work questions)
- I need to make you aware that your attendance is approaching our review threshold
- Is there anything we can do to support you to improve your attendance?

**Actions:**
- ‚úÖ Complete self-certification form
- ‚úÖ **Clearly advise** that further absences may trigger formal Attendance Review
- ‚úÖ Explain the threshold (e.g., "This is your 2nd absence in 6 months - at 3 absences we'll need to have a formal review")
- ‚úÖ Offer support (EAP, OH referral if health-related)
- ‚úÖ **Document clearly** that you've advised them of the threshold
- ‚úÖ Be empathetic but clear about policy

### Scenario 6: Threshold Already Met - Attendance Review Required

**Key Questions:**
- (All standard return to work questions)
- Your attendance has now met the threshold for a formal Attendance Review
- Can we schedule that meeting for [date/time]?

**Actions:**
- ‚úÖ Complete self-certification form
- ‚úÖ **Arrange formal Attendance Review meeting**
- ‚úÖ Send written invitation to employee (advise of right to be accompanied)
- ‚úÖ Prepare attendance record for Attendance Review
- ‚úÖ Complete Attendance Review Meeting Checklist at that meeting
- ‚úÖ Offer support and explore interventions

## Types of Adjustments to Discuss

### Temporary Adjustments (Short-term)

| Adjustment | When Appropriate | Example Duration |
|-----------|------------------|------------------|
| **Phased Return** | After long-term absence, surgery, serious illness | 2-4 weeks, building from 50% to 100% hours |
| **Lighter Duties** | Recovering from injury, post-surgery | Until fit note expires or OH advises |
| **Altered Hours** | Medical appointments, treatment schedule | Duration of treatment + recovery |
| **Reduced Hours** | Gradual return after illness | Temporary, reviewed weekly |
| **Working from Home** | Mobility issues, recovering from illness | 1-4 weeks or as needed |

### Long-term/Permanent Adjustments

| Adjustment | When Appropriate | Requires |
|-----------|------------------|----------|
| **Flexible Working** | Ongoing health condition, caring responsibilities | Formal flexible working request |
| **Ergonomic Equipment** | Musculoskeletal condition, disability | OH recommendation, workstation assessment |
| **Job Redesign** | Disability, permanent health restriction | OH advice, discussion with HR |
| **Hybrid Working** | Long-term health condition, disability | Agreement with manager, formal arrangement |
| **Assistive Technology** | Visual impairment, learning difficulty | Access to Work assessment |

## Best Practice Tips

### ‚úÖ DO

- **Create a welcoming atmosphere** - Employee may be anxious about returning
- **Be empathetic and supportive** - Show genuine concern for their wellbeing
- **Listen actively** - Give them space to explain their situation
- **Focus on support first** - Before discussing thresholds or concerns
- **Be specific about adjustments** - Clear on what, when, how long
- **Confirm in writing** - Especially for adjustments or further actions
- **Maintain confidentiality** - Don't share medical details unnecessarily
- **Follow up** - Check how they're doing in first few days back
- **Document thoroughly** - For audit trail and future reference
- **Be consistent** - Apply policy fairly across all employees

### ‚ùå DON'T

- **Interrogate** - This isn't a disciplinary meeting
- **Express doubt** - Even if you suspect the absence wasn't genuine
- **Diagnose** - You're not a medical professional
- **Make assumptions** - About their health or reasons for absence
- **Rush the meeting** - Take adequate time for discussion
- **Discuss in public** - Ensure privacy and confidentiality
- **Compare to others** - Each case is individual
- **Make promises you can't keep** - About adjustments or support
- **Forget to document** - Record the discussion and any agreements
- **Skip the meeting** - Required for every absence, no matter how short

## Red Flags - Escalate Immediately

| Red Flag | Action Required |
|----------|----------------|
| üö® **Employee returns but clearly unwell** | Don't allow them to work; suggest they see GP; send home if necessary |
| üö® **Employee mentions thoughts of self-harm** | Immediate referral to PAM Assist; inform your manager and HR |
| üö® **Work-related injury revealed** | Follow H&S incident reporting immediately |
| üö® **Disclosure of serious workplace issue** (bullying, harassment) | Follow appropriate policy; inform HR immediately |
| üö® **Employee refuses to discuss absence** | Note refusal; seek HR advice on next steps |
| üö® **Inconsistencies in explanation** vs. fit note | Don't challenge directly; seek HR advice |
