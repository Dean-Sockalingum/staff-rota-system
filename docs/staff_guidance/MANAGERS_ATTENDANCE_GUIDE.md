## Why Manage Attendance?

Sickness Absence is one of the Council's **Statutory Performance Indicators** and is used for benchmarking and performance review.

### The Cost of Poor Attendance Management

High levels of sickness absence are costly. If you don't manage attendance properly, it could:

| Impact Area | Consequences |
|------------|--------------|
| **Service Delivery** | Detrimental impact on support provided to citizens who rely on Council services |
| **Reputation** | Damage to Council's public image and trust |
| **Staff Morale** | Additional pressures affecting morale, stress levels, and engagement |
| **Culture** | Staff with intermittent absences may think it's acceptable to be absent regardless of circumstances |
| **Long-term Absent Staff** | Employees feeling cut off, out of touch, and isolated |

### The Benefits of Proactive Attendance Management

Taking an active role in managing attendance allows you to:

- ‚úÖ **Support employees** with health problems at an early stage
- ‚úÖ **Create a wellbeing culture** across the Council
- ‚úÖ **Respond effectively** to service delivery problems
- ‚úÖ **Reduce impact** on colleagues who continue to provide services
- ‚úÖ **Increase productivity** through early intervention
- ‚úÖ **Reduce costs** associated with absence
- ‚úÖ **Plan workloads** more effectively
- ‚úÖ **Improve customer satisfaction** through consistent service

### The Importance of Early Discussions

Adopting a proactive approach requires **open and honest discussions** about health conditions affecting employees' ability to perform their duties. Ensuring discussions take place at an early stage:

- Identifies adjustments that could support staff
- Delivers improved employee health and wellbeing
- Provides the right help and support when needed
- Enables better planning and workload management

## Your Role and Responsibilities

As a manager, **you have a key role** to play in monitoring and managing employees' attendance.

### Core Responsibilities

You should:

#### 1. **Be Proactive**
- Manage the health and wellbeing of your team
- Establish a positive working environment where employees can raise concerns early
- Fulfill your legal duty of care to protect employees from risks of ill health/injury at work

#### 2. **Communicate**
- Communicate Council and service attendance targets to employees
- Explain the impact of poor attendance on services and colleagues

#### 3. **Monitor**
- Regularly review attendance levels within your section or team

#### 4. **Treat Fairly**
- Treat employees fairly and consistently across all attendance matters

#### 5. **Encourage Early Help**
- Encourage employees to seek help early if they have issues affecting health or attendance
- Promote Employee Assistance Provider (EAP) services
- Consider Occupational Health referrals when appropriate

### When You Must Take Action

You are **required** to take management action when an employee:

- ‚ùå Has unauthorised absence
- ‚ùå Fails to comply with absence reporting procedures
- ‚ùå Abuses the Council's sick pay provisions
- ‚ùå Demonstrates fraudulent conduct
- ‚ùå Has an unacceptable and unsustainable attendance record

### What You Should Do When Managing Attendance

‚úÖ **Ensure Awareness**
- Make sure employees know and follow correct reporting procedures
- Confirm what forms they need to submit (e.g., fit notes)

‚úÖ **Conduct Discussions**
- Discuss all periods of absence with employees
- Carry out return to work discussions
- Conduct Attendance Reviews as required

‚úÖ **Encourage Support**
- Encourage employees to seek appropriate help early
- Promote the wide range of support services available

‚úÖ **Consider Alternatives**
- Consider other leave arrangements (Bereavement Leave, Dependents Leave)
- Refer to Leave Conditions of Service

‚úÖ **Consider Adjustments**
- Consider reasonable adjustments (temporary or permanent) to support employees

‚úÖ **Communicate Concerns**
- Advise employees of your concerns about attendance levels
- Outline potential consequences if appropriate

‚úÖ **Use Professional Services**
- Arrange referrals to Occupational Health as appropriate

‚úÖ **Document Everything**
- Provide written outcomes of discussions
- Record all action points

### Other Consequences to Address

Be mindful of other consequences of absence:

- üòî Demotivated colleagues taking on extra responsibilities
- üìâ Disruption to productivity and service delivery

- ü©∫ The nature of their illness or disability

## Absence Reporting

### Reporting Absent (Day 1)

If an employee is ill and can't attend work, they **must contact you as soon as possible** on the first day.

#### How to Report

- ‚òéÔ∏è **Direct telephone contact** with you
- üìû **Leave a message** with contact details if you're unavailable

#### If You're Unavailable

Where you've been unavailable and the employee has:

- Left a message with a colleague
- Left a recorded message on answerphone
- Sent an email or text message

**You should make a return call at the earliest opportunity**

### Questions You Should Ask (Day 1 Call)

When an employee reports sick, establish the current situation by asking:

| Question | Why It's Important |
|----------|-------------------|
| **What is the reason for the absence?** | Understand the nature of the issue |
| **What is the expected duration?** | Plan coverage and support |
| **Has medical advice been sought?** | Determine severity and next steps |
| **Is there anything you can do to help?** | Offer immediate support |
| **Are there any workplace issues contributing?** | Identify work-related causes |
| **Any outstanding work items, tasks, or upcoming meetings?** | Ensure business continuity |
| **What is their expected return date if known?** | Plan workload and coverage |
| **Are further supports required (e.g., EAP)?** | Connect to appropriate resources |

**Also ask:** Is there anything else you need to make me aware of?

**Confirm:** When they should next make contact

### Consider a Wellbeing Support Meeting

For absences due to **musculoskeletal or psychological conditions**, consider arranging a Wellbeing Support Meeting within the **first week** to establish what support can be offered.

## The Statement of Fitness for Work ("Fit Note")

The employee needs to submit a copy of **Statement of Fitness for Work** (commonly known as a "fit note") after the **7th calendar day** of absence.

### Two Types of Assessment

| Assessment | Meaning | Actions |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Not fit for work** | Employee has a health condition preventing them from working for a stated period | Cover duties; maintain contact; plan for return |
| **May be fit for work** | Employee has a condition that doesn't necessarily stop them from returning, but may not be able to do all normal duties | Discuss adjustments; consider temporary modifications; phased return options |

### "May Be Fit for Work" Options

If the fit note says "may be fit for work," it will note whether the employee would benefit from:

- üîÑ **Phased return to work** - Gradual increase in hours/days
- ‚è∞ **Altered hours** - Different start/end times or reduced hours
- üìù **Amended duties** - Temporary change to job tasks
- üè¢ **Workplace adaptations** - Environmental or equipment changes

### GP Comments

The GP or authorized healthcare professional will:

- Provide **written comments** offering a detailed view of what may help
- Explain how the condition affects what the employee can do
- Suggest things that could help the employee return to work
- State the **period of time** the advice is valid for

### Discussing the Fit Note

You should:

1. Discuss the fit note with the employee
2. Be clear about the length of time any amended duties or support will be in place
3. Note that this will be a **temporary measure** in most cases
4. Meet with the employee while they're doing adjusted duties to review progress

### When the Fit Note Expires

The fit note doesn't include an option for the GP to advise that the employee is "fully fit for work." This means **an employee is expected to return to work following expiration of their fit note**.

### Early Return to Work

If an employee wants to return to work **before the end of the fit note period** and you agree it's appropriate, **you don't need to wait** until the end of the statement period.

**However:** If you don't think it's appropriate, you may wish to seek guidance from:

- **OH Advice Line:** 0141 428 3900
- **Your Service Strategic HR team**

## Return to Work Discussions

### When Required

In line with policy, you are **required** to carry out a return to work discussion when an employee returns to work **after any period of absence**.

**Timing:** Normally on the **day of their return**

### Before the Meeting

You should have:

- ‚úÖ Details of the employee's attendance record
- ‚úÖ Familiarity with our policy
- ‚úÖ Knowledge of the thresholds for an attendance review

### Purpose of the Return to Work Discussion

The return to work discussion allows you to:

| Purpose | What to Do |
|---------|-----------|
| **Welcome back** | Confirm they're fit to be back at work |
| **Confirm and discuss** | Nature and reason for absence; likelihood of recurrence |
| **Offer support** | Provide relevant help and support |
| **Update** | Inform about developments or changes during their absence |
| **Confirm further action** | E.g., Wellbeing Support Meeting or OH referral |
| **Advise consequences** | Where attendance is giving cause for concern and reaching unsustainable level |
| **Agree actions** | If necessary, agree actions to improve attendance |
| **Complete forms** | Ensure sickness certificate form (self-certification) is completed where appropriate |

### Employee Rights

**Note:** Although this is an informal meeting which does not require notification in writing, an employee has the **right to be accompanied** by a colleague or trade union representative.

### Self Certification Form (Return to Work Form)

The purpose of the return to work form is to:

- Record details of the employee's absence
- Confirm they're fit to be back at work

**Process:**

1. Both you and the employee complete relevant sections at the meeting
2. Record whether you intend to take further action (e.g., arrange Attendance Review)
3. Both you and the employee sign the form
4. Scan and send to CBS to be kept in the employee's personal file

### Claims Against a Third Party

In cases where an employee has been absent due to an injury involving a third party (e.g., whiplash following road traffic accident):

- Ask whether they intend to make a claim against a third party
- Record these details on the sickness absence certificate

**Important:** If the employee successfully pursues a claim, the Council expects to be **reimbursed for all sickness allowance paid** for that absence.

## Managing Attendance

### Absence Categories and Equality Act Considerations

There are **two categories** of sickness absence within our policy:

| Category | Definition | Examples |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| **Short Term Intermittent Absence** | Any period of sickness absence up to 19 days | Can include absences covered by sickness absence certificate and/or fit note |
| **Long Term Absence** | Any period of absence lasting over 19 days | Extended illness, surgery recovery, long-term health conditions |

### Equality Act 2010 Requirements

Throughout the supporting attendance process, when managing either category of absence, managers **must be mindful** of the requirements of the **Equality Act 2010 (Amendment) Regulations 2023**.

The Act makes specific provisions to prevent discrimination against any employee on the grounds of a **protected characteristic**. Of particular relevance to attendance is **"disability"**.

### Definition of Disability Under the Equality Act 2010

A person is disabled if they have:

**"A physical or mental impairment which has a substantial and long-term adverse effect on their ability to carry out normal day-to-day activities"**

| Element | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| **Impairment** | May be physical or mental, or both |
| **Long-term** | Lasting at least a year, or likely to be for the rest of the person's life, or recur |
| **Substantial adverse effect** | More than minor, but may fluctuate or change, and may not be present all the time |
| **Normal day-to-day activities** | Not defined by the Act, but includes things people do in their own time; still able to fully participate in working life |

### Complex Cases

Managing disability-related sickness absence can be complex. **Consult with your Service Strategic HR teams** in relation to such situations.

üìñ **More information:** Equalities Library

## Musculoskeletal Conditions

### What are Musculoskeletal Problems?

Musculoskeletal problems may include:

- üîô Back pain
- ü¶¥ Neck and shoulder pain, including whiplash
- ‚úã Upper limb problems (shoulder, elbow, wrist, hand)
- ü¶µ Lower limb problems (groin, hip, knee, ankle, foot)

### Physiotherapy Support

A physiotherapist may help the employee to:

- Recover more quickly
- Speed up return to work
- Provide effective assessment and treatment of musculoskeletal conditions or injuries

### Criteria for Musculoskeletal Referral via OH

At the meeting, establish whether the employee meets the criteria:

‚úÖ Employee has a musculoskeletal condition **AND**  
‚úÖ Attendance is currently or likely to be affected **OR** work performance is being affected

### Priority Cases

We normally give priority to employees with a musculoskeletal problem:

- Resulting from an **industrial injury**
- Who are **absent**
- Which would **improve following treatment** and allow earlier return to work

### The Physiotherapy Report

You will receive a report from OH normally after:

- The **first session**
- The **last session**

The report will provide advice on:

- Impact of the employee's condition on their ability to perform their full range of duties
- Length of the course of treatment being provided
- Likely level of recovery
- Any reasonable adjustments or adaptations that could be made
- Employee's recovery (in the final report)

### When Physiotherapy Won't Be Provided

Physiotherapy support won't be provided in cases where the employee is **already receiving physiotherapy from another source**.

### Temporary Adjustments

You can get advice and guidance from OH on the range of duties the employee is fit to do. This information may help you make temporary adjustments to the employee's duties, which could help them return to work while still receiving treatment.

## Reduction of Annual Leave

### Working Time Regulations 1998 (as amended)

All workers are entitled to a **statutory minimum of 5.6 weeks (28 days)** paid leave each year. This can include paid public holidays.

**This entitlement is pro-rated for part-time workers.**

### When Leave is Reduced

Where an employee is absent from work through sickness absence for a **single period of over 13 weeks** in a single leave year or spanning leave years, their entitlement to leave within the leave year should be **reduced proportionately**, providing that the employee receives the **statutory minimum annual leave entitlement** in each leave year.

**Need help calculating?** Contact your Strategic HR team

### Examples

**Example 1: Jim**

- **Absence:** 3rd January to 10th April (14 weeks)
- **Service:** 7 years
- **Complete months worked:** 8 (only absent January to April)
- **Entitlement:** 19.5 days + 11.5 public holidays = **31 days**
- **Result:** ‚úÖ **Meets statutory minimum** (28 days)

**Example 2: Pam**

- **Absence:** 1st March to 2nd August
- **Complete months worked:** 5 months
- **Service:** 2 years
- **Initial calculation:** 11 days + 11.5 public holidays = 22.5 days
- **Result:** ‚ùå **Does not meet statutory minimum** (28 days)
- **Adjustment:** Adjusted to **28 days** to meet statutory minimum

### Term-Time Staff

For term-time staff, please refer to the appropriate **Management Circular**.

### Carry Forward of Untaken Statutory Leave

Where an employee is **unable to use their entitlement** to statutory leave due to sickness absence within the leave year, the employee should be allowed to **carry forward the untaken balance** of statutory leave into the next leave year.

**Important:** The carry forward only applies to the **untaken balance of statutory leave**, not the balance of untaken contractual leave provided for in the Council's conditions of service.

**Need advice?** Contact your Service Strategic HR team

## Ill Health Retiral

### Ill Health Retiral (Excluding Teachers)

Ill health retiral is **only available** to employees who are members of **Strathclyde Pension Fund**.

### Referral to OH

You'll need to make a referral to OHS to determine whether the employee meets the criteria for ill health retiral.

OHS will also assess **how likely** it is that the employee will be capable of working at some point in the future.

### Two Tiers

| Tier | Criteria | Benefits |
|------|----------|----------|
| **Tier 1** | Employee is **unlikely to work again before age 65** | Higher pension benefits |
| **Tier 2** | Employee has a **reasonable prospect of working again before 65** | Standard pension benefits |

### Granting Ill Health Retiral

Approval must be sought from an **approved qualified medical practitioner** on whether the employee meets the eligibility criteria.

**Contact your Service Strategic HR team as soon as possible** for advice.

### Appeals

An employee can **appeal** against a decision not to grant ill health retiral.

If we can't resolve the complaint, the employee can use the **Internal Disputes Resolution Procedures (IDRP)**.

**More information:**

- Strathclyde Pension Fund
- Your Service Strategic HR team

### Teachers

If the employee is a teacher, an application should be made to the **Scottish Public Pensions Agency (SPPA)**, together with supporting medical information.

**More information:**

- SPPA
- HR team within Education Services

## Capability Dismissal

### When to Consider Capability Dismissal

Where all reasonable options have been explored and the employee cannot return to work or maintain a satisfactory level of attendance, the employee should be progressed to a **lack of capability hearing**.

You may have to consider the option of **dismissal on capability grounds** where:

- ‚ùå The employee doesn't meet the criteria for ill health retiral
- ‚úÖ You have explored all options of support
- ‚ùå The employee is still unable to return to work or sustain an acceptable level of attendance

### What is Capability Dismissal?

This means that an employee's contract of employment is terminated as they're **not fit to carry out their duties** or **attend work on a regular basis** due to ill health.

**Dismissal due to ill health is a potentially fair reason for dismissal.**

### Before Considering Capability Dismissal

Before considering capability dismissal, you will have:

‚úÖ Sought advice and guidance from your **Service Strategic HR team**  
‚úÖ Met with the employee **face-to-face on a regular basis**  
‚úÖ Given the employee the **opportunity to put their side forward**  
‚úÖ Issued letters **confirming the discussions** that have taken place  
‚úÖ Sought up-to-date advice from **OHS**, where appropriate  
‚úÖ Considered **reasonable offers of support** to aid a return to work  
‚úÖ Explored options such as **phased return or lighter duties** that may enable a return to work  
‚úÖ Explored whether the employee is **eligible for ill health retiral**  
‚úÖ Advised the employee in writing of the **potential outcome of dismissal** should they be unable to improve attendance  

### Grounds for Dismissal

Capability dismissal may be appropriate in cases where:

- You can **no longer sustain the level of absence**
- You have **exhausted all other options** of support
- The employee is **unable to give an indication** of a return to work within the foreseeable future **OR** has been unable to sustain an acceptable level of attendance
- The employee **doesn't meet the criteria** for ill health retiral

### Terminal Illness

You may have to **suspend any decision to dismiss** in cases where an employee has been diagnosed as having a **terminal illness**.

**Important:** You need to consider such situations carefully and deal with them sensitively, taking the circumstances into account.

**Seek advice from:** Your Service Strategic HR team

### Inviting the Employee to the Meeting

You'll need to advise the employee **both verbally and in writing** when you have reached the point where you're considering terminating their contract on capability grounds.

**Your letter should:**

- ‚úÖ Be clear that a **potential outcome may be dismissal**
- ‚úÖ Advise the employee of their **right to be accompanied** by a colleague or trade union representative

**Template invite letter:** Your Strategic Service HR team can provide this

### Reaching a Decision

The Chair should consider **each case on its own merits** before reaching a decision.

**To help reach a decision, the following will be considered:**

- Length of the absence and whether a return to work in the foreseeable future is likely
- Reason for the absence
- Attendance history
- Effect of the disability or underlying health problem on their ability to carry out their current duties
- Effect of the absence on the service and/or section

**Advice and guidance should be sought from Strategic HR prior to a decision being made.**

### Confirming the Decision

You should confirm the employee's dismissal **in writing**, along with:

- üí∞ Final pay arrangements
- ‚öñÔ∏è Their right to **appeal** against the decision of dismissal
- üìã Where appropriate, their right to appeal against the decision **not to grant ill health retiral** from the Strathclyde Pension Scheme

### Appeals Against Dismissal

If the employee considers their dismissal unfair, they may **appeal, in writing**, to the **Head of Human Resources** within **14 days** of receiving their letter confirming the decision.

**Appeal hearing:** The **Personnel Appeals Committee**, made up of a panel of Elected Members (Councillors), will hear the appeal.

üìñ **More information:** Code of Discipline, Disciplinary and Appeals Procedure for full details of the appeals procedure

## Summary: Manager's Responsibilities at a Glance

### ‚úÖ Daily Responsibilities

- Be proactive in managing health and wellbeing
- Communicate attendance targets
- Regularly review attendance levels
- Treat employees fairly and consistently
- Encourage early help-seeking

### ‚úÖ When Employee Reports Absent (Day 1)

- Take the call or return call promptly
- Ask 8 key questions (reason, duration, medical advice, help needed, workplace issues, outstanding work, return date, EAP needed)
- Consider Wellbeing Support Meeting for musculoskeletal/psychological conditions
- Document the conversation

### ‚úÖ During Absence

- Ensure employee maintains contact (Days 4, 7, and every 7 days thereafter)
- Ensure fit notes are submitted after Day 7
- Meet face-to-face every 4-6 weeks for long-term absence
- Maintain weekly telephone contact
- Consider OH referral if absence reaches 8-12 weeks
- Provide support and keep employee updated

### ‚úÖ On Return to Work

- Conduct return to work discussion (on day of return)
- Complete sickness absence certificate (self-certification)
- Discuss nature of absence
- Offer support
- Advise of consequences if attendance reaching unsustainable level
- Consider further action (Attendance Review, OH referral)

### ‚úÖ Managing Short-Term Intermittent Absence

- Monitor thresholds (3 absences in 6 months or 5 in 12 months; 6 days in 6 months or 8 in 12 months)
- Conduct Attendance Review when thresholds met
- Offer interventions and support
- Document outcomes

### ‚úÖ Managing Long-Term Absence

- Meet around Week 4 (or earlier for musculoskeletal/psychological)
- Meet regularly every 4-6 weeks
- Consider OH referral
- Explore reasonable adjustments
- Keep employee updated
- Confirm discussions in writing
- Consider capability process if unable to return

### ‚úÖ When Unable to Return to Work

- Explore all options fully
- Consider ill health retiral
- If not eligible, consider capability dismissal
- Follow fair process
- Seek Strategic HR advice
- Document everything
